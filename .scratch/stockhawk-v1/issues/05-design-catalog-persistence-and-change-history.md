# Design catalog persistence and Change Event history

Type: grilling
Label: wayfinder:grilling
Status: resolved
Triage: ready-for-human
Blocked by: 01, 02, 13

## Question

What relational domain and persistence model preserves exact-variation Products, distinct Offers including same-Storefront duplicates, current Stock Status, Storefront identity and classifications, Catalog Snapshots and certification evidence, source provenance, crawl observations, and durable Change Events without making fast search or future alert consumption difficult? Resolve identity, lifecycle, retention, indexing, and transaction invariants.

## Comments

- **Decision 1 — Current state plus immutable history:** StockHawk will keep query-optimized current-state relational records for the latest accepted Storefront, Product, Retailer Listing, Catalog Match, classification, certification, health, and Stock Status facts. It will separately retain append-only historical and evidence records for Connector runs, observations, Catalog Snapshot membership, certification decisions, Decision Receipts, and Change Events. Normal search reads current state; future alert consumers read durable Change Events. Every accepted state transition must update current state and append its required history and event records atomically so the two cannot disagree. V1 will not use pure event sourcing or reconstruct ordinary search results by replaying history.
- **Decision 2 — Offer is a matched Retailer Listing, not a duplicate entity:** A Retailer Listing owns the durable Storefront-scoped database identity, current retailer facts, Stock Status, and Purchase Handoff target. An active Catalog Match makes that same listing an Offer; V1 will not create a second Offer identity or duplicate Offer row. Different Source Listing Identities remain separate listings and Offers even at the same Storefront with identical-looking titles, while repeat observations of the same identity update one listing idempotently. Rematching changes the Product association without replacing the listing or erasing its match history.
- **Decision 3 — Permanent internal identities with constrained source identifiers:** Every Storefront, Product, Retailer Listing, and other durable domain record receives an opaque, immutable StockHawk Identity independent of retailer-controlled names, titles, URLs, SKUs, and Connector formats. External identifiers retain their scope, scheme or issuer, provenance, and verification state. The database must ensure that one Storefront-scoped Source Listing Identity resolves to at most one Retailer Listing and that one verified official Product identifier resolves to at most one Product; titles and URLs are never global uniqueness keys. Identifier replacement, aliasing, rekeying, merging, and supersession are explicit history-preserving operations rather than silent primary-key changes.
- **Decision 4 — One current Product answer per Retailer Listing:** A Retailer Listing may have zero or one active Catalog Match, enforced by the database; many listings may match the same Product. If evidence cannot distinguish known exact variants, the one active answer may be a variant-unknown Product rather than several competing matches. Rematching atomically retires the prior active association, creates the new current association, and preserves the previous match and Decision Receipts. Connector-emitted exact sellable variants have separate Source Listing Identities and therefore remain separate Retailer Listings and Offers.
- **Decision 5 — Immutable, pinned Storefront Integration versions:** A Storefront is durable across platform and configuration changes. Each published Storefront Integration version is immutable, a Storefront has at most one active version at a time, and every Connector run, observation, Catalog Snapshot, Certification Claim, and certification decision references the exact version used. Changing coverage-affecting instructions creates and activates a new version, moves the Storefront to Needs Recertification, and preserves all prior versions and their evidence rather than rewriting history.
- **Decision 6 — Atomic, idempotent Observation Batches:** A long Connector run commits bounded batches rather than holding one crawl-wide transaction. Each batch has a run-scoped idempotency identity and atomically persists its referenced evidence and immutable observations, applies any synchronous Current State Projection transitions, appends required deterministic Change Events, and advances the Connector Checkpoint; either all of those effects commit or none do. Database uniqueness makes replay safe after an interruption. Successfully committed partial observations become searchable immediately, but run completion, Catalog Snapshot certification, and disappearance reconciliation occur only after all expected batches and the final Certification Claim succeed.
- **Decision 7 — Positive presence is immediate; absence requires certification:** A committed positive observation may immediately mark a Retailer Listing newly discovered or reappeared. Failure to observe a listing in a partial, failed, or uncertified run never changes its Listing Presence. Each certified Catalog Snapshot has immutable, uniqueness-constrained membership; publishing it compares that membership only with the previous certified snapshot for the same Storefront Integration scope. Snapshot publication, the current certified-snapshot pointer, resulting disappearance transitions, and their Change Events commit atomically. Listing Presence is independent of Stock Status: an existing out-of-stock page has not disappeared.
- **Decision 8 — Trustworthy, monotonic stock updates:** Current Stock State begins `unknown` and changes only from a conclusive Stock Observation that is newer in the listing's deterministic observation order. The observation insert, current-state update, and any status-transition Change Event commit atomically and idempotently. A repeated conclusive status refreshes its observation time without emitting a no-op event; an older delayed observation remains in history but cannot replace newer current state. Failed, omitted, or unobservable check attempts are recorded in Connector run and Health history but do not create Stock Observations, alter the last trustworthy status, or advance its `Last checked` time. Thus a known stale status remains visible with its older timestamp while Health exposes the failed attempt; `unknown` remains only until a trustworthy answer exists.
- **Decision 9 — Append-only ordered Change Event stream:** Every first discovery, reappearance, certified disappearance, and actual Stock Status transition appends an immutable, schema-versioned Change Event in the same transaction as its Current State Projection change. Each event has an opaque ID, monotonically increasing stream position, event type, durable subject IDs, previous and new values, effective observation time, recorded time, and causal run, observation, snapshot, or evidence references. A causal idempotency key prevents replay from appending the same logical event twice. Change Events are retained permanently and never carry a shared mutable `processed` flag; any future consumer owns an independent durable bookmark after a stream position and may replay safely from it.
- **Decision 10 — Tiered retention with a 30-day detailed-history default:** Durable identities and lifecycle history, Product and Retailer Listing records, source-identifier mappings, Storefront Integration versions, Catalog Match history, Decision Receipts, Change Events, current state, and compact snapshot, certification, run, observation, and evidence envelopes remain permanent. Unpinned bulky source payloads, repeated unchanged observations, verbose run diagnostics, and superseded detail remain in a configurable rolling window that defaults to 30 days, then are compacted or payload-pruned into hashes, counts, time ranges, and daily summaries without breaking causal references. Evidence required by an active certification, unresolved review or Identity Conflict, or active health investigation remains pinned; required current snapshot membership also remains pinned. Retention never deletes Products, Retailer Listings, Offers, important decisions, or events, and pruning is observable rather than a dangling or silent hard delete.
- **Decision 11 — One rebuildable Search Document per Retailer Listing:** StockHawk will maintain a versioned, controlled-denormalization search projection uniquely keyed by Retailer Listing identity. It contains searchable copies of the raw retailer title, canonical Product name and trusted aliases when matched, retailer name and site URL, plus current classification, match, Stock Status, Listing Presence, visibility, and stable navigation IDs. Normal daily search uses this projection with match-any chips and filters; one document per listing preserves every distinct Offer, including duplicate-looking same-Storefront listings. The normalized relational model remains authoritative, projection-affecting state transitions update the document transactionally, and a deterministic local rebuild can recreate every document and its full-text index without network access. The storage engine and full-text implementation remain a local-stack decision.
- **Decision 12 — Database-enforced persistence invariants:** Critical identity, cardinality, state, idempotency, and lifecycle rules are enforced by relational foreign keys, non-null and allowed-value checks, uniqueness constraints, restrictive deletion, and transaction or database rules rather than application convention alone. This includes scoped source identities, verified Product identifiers, one active Catalog Match per Retailer Listing, one active Storefront Integration version, run-scoped batch identities, unique snapshot membership, unique causal Change Events, one Search Document per listing, and the four allowed Stock Status values. Durable entities and immutable history cannot be cascade-deleted; identity merge, supersession, retirement, retention compaction, and payload pruning use explicit state transitions that preserve permanent envelopes and references. Complex multi-row invariants must be exercised through one authoritative transactional write surface and verified by persistence tests.
- **Decision 13 — Versioned cursor pagination for both search views:** Flat and Storefront-grouped search use opaque, URL-safe keyset cursors rather than deep offset scans. A cursor is bound to a version and signature of the search chips, filters, view, and sort order, and contains the last stable ordering values plus StockHawk Identity tie-breakers; a mismatched or obsolete cursor fails safely instead of continuing another query. Grouped traversal carries enough Storefront and Retailer Listing position to resume within or after a group without merging listings. This supports fast forward and backward navigation and URL persistence without server-side search sessions; arbitrary direct jumps to distant numeric pages are not a V1 requirement.
- **Decision 14 — Live search instead of frozen result sessions:** Every search page reads the latest committed Search Documents and current filters; a Search Cursor is only a position bookmark and does not freeze a private result snapshot. Stable, minimally mutable sort keys and identity tie-breakers reduce movement, but a listing whose live classification, presence, or Stock Status changes may enter or leave the matching set between pages. This is accepted in favor of immediate freshness, URL portability, and no server-side search-session storage. Restarting or refreshing from the beginning performs a new complete pass over current truth.
- **Decision 15 — Durable records transition; they are not hard-deleted:** Products, Product identity and alias records, Storefronts, Storefront Integration versions, Retailer Listings, Catalog Match and classification history, Decision Receipts, certified snapshot and certification envelopes, and Change Events remain addressable permanently. Retirement, disappearance, death, exclusion, correction, merge, and supersession are explicit timestamped and reasoned Lifecycle Transitions. A merge selects one canonical survivor and leaves an acyclic redirect from each superseded identity while remapping current associations transactionally and preserving historical references. Rare and retired Products and their real retailer listings remain searchable under the accepted visibility policy. Physical deletion is limited to uncommitted temporary data and retention-governed payload/detail compaction, never a durable domain identity or historical envelope.
- **Decision 16 — Query-shaped, benchmark-verified indexes:** Every secondary or full-text index must name the production query and ordering it serves, be inspected through the selected engine's query plan, and justify its write and storage cost under representative full load. Required query families include scoped listing upsert and identity resolution, current Product and Storefront Offers, Search Document text/filter/cursor traversal in flat and grouped views, Catalog Snapshot membership comparison, ordered Change Event consumption and causal deduplication, and the later-defined due-work and unhealthy-Storefront paths. Composite and current-row/partial indexes should follow actual equality, range, and keyset predicates; speculative single-column indexing is rejected. Performance tests enforce the accepted search budgets, and unused or redundant indexes are removed.
- **Decision 17 — One authoritative transactional persistence boundary:** Connector Adapters, matchers, certifiers, schedulers, retention jobs, review actions, and other workers never mutate domain tables directly. They submit typed, idempotent commands to one persistence module whose transaction operations own immutable evidence insertion, Current State Projection transitions, Search Document maintenance, Lifecycle Transitions, Change Event creation, checkpoint advancement, and constraint-safe retries. Collection work may run concurrently, while conflicting writes use database transactions and subject ordering rather than bypassing invariants. Read paths may use dedicated query repositories, but every write has one auditable doorway and persistence contract.
- **Decision 18 — Typed relational core plus versioned raw payloads:** Identity, relationships, standardized retailer facts, current state, classification, timestamps, provenance references, and other constrained or queried values live in normalized typed relational columns and tables. Each Retailer Listing Observation may additionally reference an immutable, schema-versioned raw payload or Source Evidence Artifact that preserves Connector-specific fields and content hashes without turning the core model into arbitrary JSON or retailer-specific columns. A newly important cross-cutting fact is promoted deliberately through a migration and local backfill or reclassification. Raw payload pruning follows Retention Class while its permanent envelope, schema version, hash, and extracted decision evidence remain.

## Answer

StockHawk uses a normalized relational source of truth with two coordinated layers: query-optimized Current State Projections for what the application believes now, and immutable or append-only evidence and history for why it believes it. They change through one Persistence Boundary and the same transaction whenever a current fact depends on a new observation or decision. Normal search never reconstructs state by replaying events, and V1 is not a pure event-sourced system.

### Relational ownership model

The physical names may follow the selected local database's conventions, but the schema must preserve these conceptual relations and ownership boundaries:

| Area | Current and identity records | Evidence and history | Required shape |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Storefront | Storefront, approved origins, current classification/health pointers | Immutable Storefront Integration versions and Lifecycle Transitions | One durable Storefront identity; at most one active Integration version |
| Master Catalog | Product, optional Product Family, current Product Authority and Product Lifecycle | Product identifiers, aliases, evidence links, authority/lifecycle transitions, merge and supersession redirects | Exact known variants are separate Products; verified official identifiers are uniquely constrained; redirects are acyclic |
| Retailer catalog | Retailer Listing, current retailer facts, Listing Presence | Storefront-scoped Source Listing Identities and aliases, Retailer Listing Observations, Raw Observation Payload and Source Evidence Artifact envelopes | One internal listing identity per scoped source identity; different source identities never merge merely because titles or URLs look alike |
| Classification | Current listing classification and zero-or-one active Catalog Match | Match/classification history and immutable Decision Receipts | An active match makes the listing an Offer; there is no duplicate Offer table or identity; many listings may match one Product |
| Stock | One Current Stock State per Retailer Listing | Conclusive Stock Observations plus compacted operational summaries | Exactly `in stock`, `out of stock`, `preorder`, or initial `unknown`; failures are run/Health facts, not stock answers |
| Runs and proof | Current run/checkpoint and current certified-snapshot pointers | Connector Runs, Observation Batches, metrics, Certification Claims and decisions, Catalog Snapshots and membership | Every record pins the exact Integration version and causal run; certified snapshot membership is immutable and unique |
| Search | One Search Document per Retailer Listing | Projection version and rebuild metadata | Derived only; normalized records remain authoritative; rebuild needs no retailer request |
| Change stream | None—the stream is history, not current state | Permanent ordered Change Events and future per-consumer bookmarks | Append-only, schema-versioned, causally deduplicated; no shared `processed` flag |

Every durable record receives an opaque StockHawk Identity. Retailer-controlled titles, URLs, SKUs, UPCs, platform IDs, and Connector-derived keys retain their scope, scheme, provenance, verification state, and identity-rule version but never become internal primary keys. A Source Listing Identity is unique within its Storefront and identity namespace. Rekeying or aliasing is explicit and preserves both identities and their evidence.

Offer is a role, not another stored copy. A Retailer Listing with an active Catalog Match is an Offer and uses the listing's identity, stock state, retailer URL, and Purchase Handoff. Two same-Storefront listings with different Source Listing Identities remain two Offers even if every displayed field is identical. A listing has at most one active Product answer; ambiguity uses one variant-unknown Product rather than simultaneous guesses, while prior matches remain historical.

The core schema uses typed normalized columns and relations for every constrained or queried fact. Connector-specific extras live in schema-versioned Raw Observation Payloads attached to immutable observation or evidence envelopes. Promoting a newly useful fact requires a migration and local backfill or reclassification rather than arbitrary JSON queries or retailer-specific core columns.

### Authoritative transactions

All writers submit typed commands with idempotency identities to one Persistence Boundary. Connector Adapters, matchers, certifiers, schedulers, retention jobs, and review actions never update tables directly. Collection may run concurrently; the boundary orders conflicting subject transitions, rejects stale current-state writes, and owns these transaction recipes:

1. **Commit an Observation Batch.** Insert or reuse the run-scoped batch identity, evidence envelopes, immutable observations, and any new scoped source identities; apply eligible current listing, presence, and stock transitions; update affected Search Documents; append causally unique Change Events; and advance the opaque Connector Checkpoint. All effects commit or none do. Replay returns the already-committed result instead of duplicating rows or events.
2. **Apply classification or matching.** Persist the Decision Receipt, create or strengthen a Product when allowed, retire the prior current classification or Catalog Match, activate at most one new match, update lifecycle/redirect facts and the Search Document, and append any required discovery event in one transaction. Historical associations never change meaning.
3. **Advance stock.** Insert the conclusive Stock Observation. Only a deterministically newer observation may replace Current Stock State. The same status refreshes `Last checked` without a no-op event; a real transition updates current state and appends its Change Event atomically. Older results remain historical. Failed, omitted, or unobservable checks update run and Health evidence only, preserving both status and its older timestamp.
4. **Publish certification.** Validate the final claim and complete batch set, seal immutable and uniqueness-constrained Catalog Snapshot membership, activate the new current certified-snapshot pointer, compare it with the prior certified snapshot, transition absent listings to disappeared, update Search Documents, and append disappearance events in one transaction. Positive evidence may mark discovery or reappearance earlier; partial, failed, and uncertified runs can never infer absence.
5. **Merge, supersede, or retire.** Select one canonical survivor where applicable, create a reasoned timestamped Lifecycle Transition and acyclic redirect, remap only current associations, and rebuild affected Search Documents while all historical foreign keys continue to identify the records that existed at the time.
6. **Compact retained detail.** After eligibility and pin checks, replace rolling detail with permanent hashes, counts, time ranges, daily summaries, and causal envelopes, then mark payload state and pruning reason. Compaction never leaves a dangling Decision Receipt, event, snapshot, certification, or run reference.

Each historical fact distinguishes effective or observed time from database-recorded time and retains its causal run, batch, observation, snapshot, decision, and evidence references as applicable. Current projections use deterministic observation ordering or a state revision so a delayed worker cannot overwrite newer truth.

### Database-enforced invariants

The database is the final safety net in addition to application validation. Foreign keys, non-null and allowed-value checks, uniqueness constraints, restrictive deletion rules, and transaction-level or database rules enforce at least:

- valid Stock Status, classification, lifecycle, run, certification, and retention states;
- one active Storefront Integration version per Storefront;
- one Retailer Listing per scoped Source Listing Identity and one Search Document per listing;
- one active Catalog Match per listing and referentially valid Product targets;
- one Product per verified official identifier unless an authoritative identifier-alias record proves equivalence;
- one committed batch per run-scoped idempotency identity and one observation per run/item identity;
- one membership row per Catalog Snapshot and Retailer Listing;
- one logical Change Event per causal transition and a unique ordered stream position; and
- no cascade deletion of durable identities, decisions, evidence envelopes, or history.

Impossible multi-row transitions use the authoritative transaction operations rather than repository-per-table writes. Persistence tests must deliberately attempt duplicates, stale writes, conflicting matches, replay, illegal lifecycle changes, and destructive deletion and prove the database rejects them.

### Search and query paths

The Search Document is controlled denormalization keyed by Retailer Listing. It copies the raw retailer title, matched canonical Product name and trusted aliases, retailer name and site URL, current classification and match status, Current Stock State, Listing Presence, visibility, and stable navigation identities. Offers and Provisional Candidates can therefore follow the accepted default visibility while Excluded and Unclassified Listings remain locally queryable through their appropriate operational surfaces.

Flat and Storefront-grouped views use versioned, URL-safe keyset cursors bound to the query chips, filters, view, and sort signature. Stable ordering values plus Storefront and Retailer Listing identity tie-breakers resume within or after a group without deduplicating listings. Results remain live: the cursor is a bookmark, not a frozen server-side search session, so a current-state change may move a listing between pages; restarting from the beginning is a new authoritative pass. Arbitrary distant numeric-page jumps are not required in V1.

Secondary and full-text indexes are query-shaped. Every index must name its production query, be inspected with the chosen engine's query planner, and prove its value under representative full load. The required families are scoped source-identity upsert, current Product/Storefront Offers, Search Document text plus filter and cursor traversal, snapshot membership comparison, ordered event consumption and causal deduplication, and the later-defined due-work and unhealthy-Storefront queries. Speculative and redundant indexes are rejected because they waste disk and slow crawl writes. The chosen stack must demonstrate the accepted search latency targets with realistic row counts and worst-case filters.

### Change Events and retention

First discovery, reappearance, certified disappearance, and actual Stock Status transitions append schema-versioned Change Events in the same transaction as current state. Each event has an opaque ID, monotonic stream position, event type, durable subject identities, previous and new values, effective observation time, recorded time, and causal references. A stable causal idempotency key makes job replay harmless. Events are retained permanently. Future email, SMS, or other consumers keep independent durable bookmarks after a stream position and may replay; they never mutate or delete the source event.

Permanent storage includes all durable identities, source-identifier mappings, Integration versions, current state, lifecycle and match history, Decision Receipts, Change Events, and compact observation, run, snapshot, certification, and evidence envelopes. Unpinned raw payloads, verbose diagnostics, repeated unchanged observations, and superseded detail use a configurable rolling window defaulting to 30 days. Afterward they compact to hashes and summaries. Evidence needed by an active certification, unresolved review or Identity Conflict, active health investigation, or current snapshot comparison remains pinned until superseded or resolved. Retention never removes a Product, Retailer Listing, Offer identity, rare legacy record, important decision, or event.

The local-stack frontier chooses the database engine, full-text mechanism, payload location, migration tooling, backup implementation, and process topology. Those choices must implement this model without weakening its constraints, transactions, rebuildability, retention, or measured query paths.
