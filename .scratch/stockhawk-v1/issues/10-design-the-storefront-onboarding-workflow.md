# Design the one-pass Storefront onboarding workflow

Type: grilling
Label: wayfinder:grilling
Status: resolved
Triage: ready-for-human
Blocked by: 02, 03, 04, 07

## Question

What exact one-pass workflow should combine Computer Use inspection with Storefront Integration implementation so every Candidate Site is visited once, resolved to its real commerce destination, classified as live/Dormant/Dead/Non-Store, attached to the strongest Connector, exhaustively verified for Jellycat coverage, and supplied with durable evidence and repair context? Define inputs, operator decisions, outputs, retry/escalation paths, and when custom code is justified.

## Comments

- **Decision — one durable case per Candidate Site:** One resumable Onboarding Case owns each Candidate Site from its first inspection through a terminal Candidate resolution. Pauses for retry, research, or Adapter implementation retain the same evidence and progress rather than restarting the audit. Multiple Candidate Sites may resolve to one existing Storefront; their cases link to it, while Integration and certification work occurs once for that Storefront.
- **Decision — every case receives browser and network inspection:** The initial Storefront Audit uses Computer Use for human-style navigation and captures a sanitized Playwright/DevTools network trace during the same visit. Basic tracing is universal; deeper endpoint, pagination, variant, and stock inspection is conditional when the strongest repeatable catalog method is unclear. Evidence never retains cookies or sensitive headers. The resulting Connector remains HTTP-first and receives a Browser Access Grant only when browser execution is proven necessary.
- **Decision — bounded preflight prepares the visit:** Before Computer Use, an Onboarding Preflight first checks local Storefront-origin aliases and prior Candidate evidence, then uses the Crawl Request Broker for a bounded reachability, redirect, sitemap, and platform-surface probe. It prepares likely routes and duplicate links for the human-style audit but never skips that audit or assigns a Candidate resolution, Storefront disposition, Adapter, or Catalog Certification.
- **Decision — investigation is on demand, not recurring collection:** Onboarding Preflight, Computer Use, deep DevTools inspection, network tracing, and human resolution run only for a new Onboarding Case or explicit re-audit/repair. Terminal onboarding closes browser contexts; disposable experiments are removed or promoted into maintained Adapter code and tests; retained evidence follows its Retention Class. Reusable onboarding tooling remains installed but dormant. Normal scheduled work invokes only the published Storefront Integration's Connector Adapter for Catalog Discovery and Stock Monitoring.
- **Decision — both Connector jobs receive an onboarding proof run:** A live Storefront cannot finish successful onboarding from a catalog result alone. Its proposed Integration must earn Catalog Certification through a complete Catalog Discovery and then run Stock Monitoring against every currently confirmed eligible Offer, accounting for each target. A conclusive status is preferred, but retained evidence of intrinsic source unobservability may honestly leave a target `unknown` and visibly degrade monitoring rather than inventing a status or trapping the Candidate resolution forever.
- **Decision — machine stock signals require shopper-visible validation:** Before an Integration may treat any API, feed, embedded state, or markup field as Stock Status evidence, onboarding compares that signal contemporaneously with the exact selected variant's rendered public product-page state across every distinct availability condition currently observable at the Storefront. A contradiction disqualifies the machine signal; `available` or `availableForSale` alone never proves `in stock`. The audit seeks a different shopper-equivalent signal, otherwise records `unknown`; it never tests carts, checkout, shipping eligibility, or private inventory. This is one centrally enforced Connector conformance rule for every Platform and Bespoke Connector Adapter. Each Storefront Integration must supply its own proof because themes, apps, locale, and retailer configuration can change semantics even when two Storefronts share a platform Adapter.
- **Decision — stock semantics receive rotating revalidation:** Every Storefront Integration runs a small Stock Semantics Sentinel during periodic Catalog Discovery, rotating exact-variant examples rather than reopening the full Storefront Audit or adding a browser visit to every stock check. It re-compares the trusted machine signal with the contemporaneous shopper-visible page through the Crawl Request Broker and also runs after relevant surface drift. A confirmed contradiction stops accepting new observations from that signal, preserves prior trustworthy Current Stock State, degrades the stock-monitoring dimension, and triggers re-audit; Catalog Certification remains intact unless the change also affects catalog coverage.
- **Decision — Candidate-to-Storefront resolution is many-to-many:** One Storefront Audit follows every clearly retailer-controlled public commerce destination reached from its Candidate Site. Redirects, aliases, and origins proven to expose one catalog and stock system become approved origins of one Storefront; destinations with meaningfully distinct catalogs, regions, or stock become separate Storefronts and Integrations. Several Candidate Sites may resolve to any one of them without duplicate onboarding. Unrelated marketplaces, directories, advertisements, and retailer discovery beyond the supplied or clearly retailer-controlled path remain out of scope.
- **Decision — ambiguous Storefront identities remain separate:** Redirect/canonical evidence, stable platform shop identity, source listing identities and catalog fingerprints, plus stock-system semantics may prove that origins belong to one Storefront. Similar names, domains, themes, or overlapping merchandise cannot. When evidence is inconclusive, StockHawk keeps separate Storefront identities and work rather than risk missing catalog or stock differences; stronger later evidence may perform one explicit Lifecycle Transition merge while preserving every Candidate link, observation, decision, and prior identity redirect.
- **Decision — custom code follows an evidence-gated ladder:** Onboarding first tries a compatible registered Platform Connector Adapter with schema-validated configuration. A missing behavior that represents a reusable platform capability extends that shared Adapter; a genuinely Storefront-specific but stable public mechanism becomes a Bespoke Connector Adapter behind the same Connector interface. Before maintained custom code begins, a bounded disposable proof must identify a plausible V1-allowed route to exhaustive parent/variant enumeration, shopper-validated stock, and safe repeatability through the Crawl Request Broker. Temporary failures, configuration mistakes, CAPTCHA/private access, and methods without credible closure evidence never justify retailer-name branches or brittle bypass code; they follow retry, repair, Partial, or strict Blocked handling instead.
- **Decision — the rollout is agent-led without per-site owner approval:** During execution, Codex owns the full Onboarding Case queue: Computer Use inspection, network analysis, Storefront resolution, Integration configuration, Adapter implementation, certification, stock validation, retries, and evidence-backed terminal outcomes. Technical ambiguity remains agent investigation work rather than owner homework. The owner is interrupted only when new authority or a genuine product/scope preference is required; ordinary Adapter choices, difficult sites, terminal classifications, and Connector repairs require no per-Storefront sign-off.
- **Decision — completion produces one exhaustive closeout:** After the full rollout meets the V1 completion contract, Codex delivers an Onboarding Closeout Report reconciling the immutable input through every Candidate Site and Storefront. It summarizes Platform and Bespoke Adapters made or changed, Integrations published, catalog and stock proof, duplicates/aliases, and every Dead, Non-Store, Dormant, or Blocked outcome with reasons and evidence links. It uses precise outcomes rather than one ambiguous `rejected` bucket; no unaccounted or Partial case may be hidden in a completed report.
- **Decision — the closeout is generated, not hand reconciled:** The report is deterministically generated from the immutable Seed Site Record provenance, Onboarding Cases, current domain records, Decision Receipts, verification facts, and linked evidence. It provides a concise human-readable report plus exhaustive CSV ledgers for row-level Candidate, Storefront, Integration, Adapter, classification, and rejected-method reconciliation. Counts must balance back to the supplied workbook, and regeneration from the same committed state must produce the same substantive result.
- **Decision — closure requires every discovered Storefront branch:** An Onboarding Case that discovers several distinct retailer-controlled Storefronts remains open until every branch reaches an accepted terminal outcome or links to the one other case that owns the same shared Storefront work. A certified branch cannot hide a Partial sibling. Duplicate links prevent repeated crawling, but the overall rollout and closeout still wait for the shared Storefront's owning work to finish; ownership links are acyclic and uniquely identify that work.
- **Decision — difficult cases suspend locally, never globally:** Every nonterminal Onboarding Case persists its exact workflow stage, Connector Checkpoint, evidence, stable wait reason, attempted methods, next eligible action, and shared dependency before yielding the worker. The queue continues with other Candidates. Cases needing one reusable Adapter improvement coalesce behind the same repair work and all resume after its verified publication; a Bespoke Adapter blocks only its owning Storefront branches. Resume never repeats a completed browser audit unless new evidence or surface drift specifically requires re-audit.
- **Decision — first-time recurring scheduling has a proof gate:** A newly discovered Storefront runs only bounded onboarding, conformance, discovery, retry, and validation jobs until its Integration earns Catalog Certification, passes per-Storefront Stock Semantics Validation, and completes one Stock Monitoring accounting pass. Observations committed before that gate remain stored and searchable but carry no normal freshness promise. Passing the gate publishes normal lifecycle scheduling and ends browser inspection. A previously proven Storefront whose later discovery becomes Partial keeps its independently valid Stock Monitoring schedule unless stock evidence or Integration Drift invalidates that method.

## Answer

“One pass” means one durable, resumable Onboarding Case owns each Candidate Site and every distinct Storefront branch it reveals; it does not mean one uninterrupted browser session. Codex drives the queue without per-site owner approval, and the case preserves its work through retries, shared Adapter implementation, and certification. Multiple cases may link to one uniquely owned Storefront branch so the Storefront is integrated and crawled once.

### Workflow

1. **Open the case from immutable evidence.** Attach every source Seed Site Record, normalization decision, known-origin alias, Legacy Connector Label, and current Connector Registry version. The case has one durable identity, explicit stage, owner, evidence links, attempts, dependencies, and next action.
2. **Run local-first Onboarding Preflight.** Search existing Storefront origins and prior decisions before spending network capacity. Then use the Crawl Request Broker for bounded reachability, redirect, sitemap, and public platform-surface probes. Preflight supplies clues only; it cannot classify the Candidate, select an Adapter, or certify coverage.
3. **Perform one browser-assisted Storefront Audit.** Computer Use follows the Candidate URL, retailer-controlled redirects, and clearly owned shop links while Playwright/DevTools captures a sanitized network trace. Record the redirect chain, ownership evidence, screenshots or decisive rendered states, public origins, platform and catalog fingerprints, candidate enumeration/stock routes, and rejected methods. Never retain cookies, authorization, unrestricted headers, or other secrets.
4. **Resolve the commerce topology.** One Candidate may reveal zero, one, or several Storefronts, and several Candidates may link to one Storefront. Proven aliases of one catalog and stock system become approved origins of one Storefront; meaningfully different catalogs, regions, or stock systems remain distinct. Ambiguity defaults to separate identities. Unrelated marketplaces, directories, advertisements, and retailer discovery beyond the supplied or clearly controlled path are ignored.
5. **Qualify the strongest allowed Connector method.** Prefer an existing compatible Platform Connector Adapter with schema-validated Storefront Integration options. If a missing capability generalizes, extend the shared Adapter; if a stable public mechanism is genuinely unique, create a Bespoke Connector Adapter. Maintained custom code begins only after a bounded proof shows a plausible route to exhaustive parent/variant enumeration, shopper-validated stock, and broker-safe repetition. Configuration mistakes, transient failures, private access, CAPTCHA, or evasion do not justify brittle code.
6. **Create and prove the proposed Integration.** Pin approved origins, catalog roots, locale, Adapter/configuration version, expected surface fingerprint, Certification Recipe, pacing hints, and any narrowly audited Browser Access Grant. Run complete Catalog Discovery over the entire observable public catalog—not Jellycat search results—and persist bounded observations immediately. The central matcher classifies locally; the central Catalog Certifier alone decides whether closure, counts, variants, and snapshot evidence earn certification. Useful incomplete work remains Partial and the case stays open.
7. **Validate stock semantics and monitoring.** For every Platform and Bespoke Adapter, each Storefront Integration independently compares candidate machine signals with contemporaneous rendered product-page state for the exact selected variant across every availability condition currently observable. A raw `available`-style boolean never proves `in stock`; contradiction disqualifies the signal, and no trustworthy replacement yields `unknown`. Then run Stock Monitoring once against every confirmed eligible Offer and account for every target. Cart, checkout, shipping eligibility, credentials, and private inventory remain out of scope.
8. **Cross the publication gate.** A first-time Storefront enters normal recurring Catalog Discovery and Stock Monitoring only after Catalog Certification, per-Integration Stock Semantics Validation, and the initial monitoring accounting pass. Earlier observations remain searchable without a normal freshness promise. Passing the gate closes browser resources and leaves only the published Integration and maintained Adapter in scheduled use. Later Partial discovery at a previously proven Storefront does not stop independently valid Stock Monitoring.

### Outcomes and nonblocking recovery

An Onboarding Case is complete only when all of its Storefront branches are accounted for as certified active, certified zero-Jellycat Dormant, strictly evidenced Blocked, strictly evidenced Dead, Non-Store, or linked to the one case owning an existing Storefront. Partial, missing Adapter code, unresolved identity, and ordinary failure are nonterminal. Dormant requires a certified complete zero-Jellycat snapshot; empty search results never suffice.

The accepted Health policy governs evidence thresholds: throttling changes capacity rather than classification; transient work follows broker backoff; structural configuration or Adapter faults enter Repair Required; Blocked requires separated reproduction of one external barrier after every allowed Integration method; and Dead requires affirmative permanent closure or three fresh audits spanning at least 30 days. A suspended case retains its exact stage, Connector Checkpoint, evidence, stable reason, attempted methods, next eligible action, and shared repair dependency while the rest of the queue continues. One shared Adapter repair wakes every dependent case; one Bespoke repair blocks only its Storefront branches.

After publication, a bounded Stock Semantics Sentinel rotates exact-variant comparisons during periodic Catalog Discovery and after relevant drift. Confirmed contradiction suspends that machine signal, preserves prior trustworthy Current Stock State, degrades Stock Monitoring, and queues re-audit. It does not reopen the full audit on every stock check or invalidate Catalog Certification unless catalog coverage also changed.

### Closeout

Onboarding and deep inspection tooling remain reusable but dormant after cases finish; disposable probes are deleted or promoted into maintained Adapter code and tests, and evidence follows its Retention Class. The rollout is not complete while any Candidate or Storefront branch is unaccounted or Partial.

At completion, Codex generates one Onboarding Closeout Report from committed provenance, Onboarding Cases, Decision Receipts, domain state, verification facts, and evidence links. A concise readable report plus exhaustive CSV ledgers reconcile every source row, Candidate, Storefront, Integration, Adapter made or changed, catalog/stock proof, alias or merge, rejected method, and Dead, Non-Store, Dormant, or Blocked outcome. Counts balance to the immutable workbook, and regeneration from the same committed state produces the same substantive report.
