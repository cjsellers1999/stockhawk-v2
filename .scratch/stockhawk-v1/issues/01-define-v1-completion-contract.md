# Define the V1 completion contract

Type: grilling
Label: wayfinder:grilling
Status: resolved
Triage: ready-for-human
Blocked by:

## Question

What measurable conditions must all be true before StockHawk V1 is accepted, covering Candidate Site outcomes, Catalog Certification and Partial visibility, search responsiveness, Stock Status freshness disclosure, Health Page diagnostics, private Mac mini operation, manual purchase handoff, and alert-ready Change Events? Produce one coherent acceptance contract plus explicit non-goals using the established domain language.

## Comments

- Product-identification signals supplied in conversation are hypotheses, not predetermined architecture. Independent primary-source research is in progress at `../research/jellycat-master-catalog-and-identification.md`.
- The user should not be required to manually provide every Jellycat Product unless the research demonstrates that no reliable official or repeatable source exists.
- Search, filtering, view changes, and pagination must display results within 500 ms; the initial page must be usable within 2 seconds over the private home network under the representative V1 data load.
- An Offer retains its latest observed Stock Status regardless of age. Every result discloses `Last checked`; overdue or failing monitoring is surfaced through Storefront Health rather than rewriting Stock Status to `unknown`.
- Primary-source research completed at [`Jellycat Master Catalog and Product Identification Research`](../research/jellycat-master-catalog-and-identification.md). It supports an open-world Master Catalog built from official regional catalogs, exact variants, historical official PDFs, and provisional retailer discoveries; no user-maintained exhaustive list is required.
- V1 must automatically bootstrap and refresh the Master Catalog from official regional catalogs, exact variants, New/Coming Soon surfaces, periodic complete catalog scans, and Provisional Candidates discovered at retailers. Manual input is limited to ambiguous exception review.
- The initial exhaustive official Master Catalog bootstrap must complete before the first large Storefront crawl. After bootstrap, official Master Catalog Discovery, Storefront Catalog Discovery, and Stock Monitoring continue as independent recurring loops; absence from the Master Catalog never suppresses a Provisional Candidate.
- Search must include confirmed Offers and Provisional Candidates by default under `All potential Jellycats`, with URL-persistent `Confirmed only` and `Provisional only` filters. Every Provisional Candidate must display its unresolved reason and collected evidence.
- Every active Storefront must have separate fastest-measured-safe targets for Stock Monitoring and full Catalog Discovery. V1 must meet both under the representative total workload or surface the delay through Storefront Health.
- The scheduler may crawl multiple different Storefronts concurrently within a measured global residential-IP/Mac mini limit, but only one job may run per Storefront at a time. Overlapping discovery and monitoring work must be coalesced when one Connector request can satisfy both.
- V1 requires a dedicated operational Health Page covering every Candidate Site and Storefront. It must separate health, Catalog Certification, and freshness; show Connector/URL, last attempt/success, next check, delay, counts and suspicious drops, failures/latest actionable error, and certification evidence; and provide safe retry, pause/resume, review, and diagnostic-detail actions.
- V1 is a private, single-owner application: one local admin login protects the UI and Health Page controls, multi-user accounts and roles are out of scope, and the application must not be exposed directly to the public internet.
- After a Mac mini or application restart, StockHawk must start automatically, preserve stored data, and safely resume unfinished crawling without creating duplicate jobs or requiring manual repair.
- StockHawk must create an automatic database backup at least daily, retain the latest seven daily backups, and pass a documented restore test before V1 acceptance.
- V1 must durably record a Change Event whenever a Product or Retailer Listing is first discovered, disappears, or reappears, and whenever a listing's Stock Status changes. Each event preserves the previous and new values, observation time, and source crawl; sending alerts is explicitly deferred.
- Every result must provide a one-click Purchase Handoff to its exact retailer listing. Adding to carts, retailer authentication, checkout, and automated purchasing are explicitly outside V1.
- V1 cannot finish with an unaudited or unexplained Candidate Site. Each Candidate Site must resolve to a certified active or Dormant Storefront, a Dead Storefront, a Non-Store Candidate, or a formally Blocked Storefront with reproducible evidence.
- A Partial Storefront is temporary and remains in investigation and reverification until Catalog Certification is earned. Difficulty or incomplete verification alone cannot turn Partial into Blocked; Blocked requires a specific reproducible external access barrier using allowed methods.
- Each retailer-listed variant is preserved as its own Retailer Listing and search result, even when the retailer or another Storefront exposes a duplicate-looking Product.
- Price history and price comparison are not V1 requirements. A current price may be shown when normal Catalog Discovery already provides it, but StockHawk makes no extra request solely for price and never hides a listing because price is missing.
- V1 must operate within one residential IP address and the Mac mini's measured safe capacity. Proxy rotation, CAPTCHA bypass, anti-bot evasion, and private or login-restricted catalog access are not allowed ways to achieve coverage.

## Answer

StockHawk V1 is accepted only when all of the following conditions pass under the complete representative V1 workload:

1. **Every supplied site is accounted for.** The immutable workbook is cleaned and deduplicated before seeding, every Candidate Site is audited, and each resolves to a certified active or Dormant Storefront, a Dead Storefront, a Non-Store Candidate, or a formally Blocked Storefront with reproducible evidence. Partial Storefronts remain in investigation and cannot count as finished merely because certification is difficult.
2. **Catalog coverage is evidence-based.** Catalog Certification accounts for every reachable public product route and exact sellable variant at a Storefront and records its method, timestamp, scope, counts, and supporting evidence. It does not claim hidden, private, login-restricted, or otherwise unobservable merchandise. Partial results remain searchable and visibly incomplete.
3. **New Jellycats are discovered automatically.** An initial Master Catalog bootstrap from official regional catalogs completes before the large retailer crawl. Official New/Coming Soon surfaces and periodic complete official scans keep it open-world afterward. An unfamiliar retailer listing becomes a visible Provisional Candidate instead of being discarded; the owner does not maintain an exhaustive manual list.
4. **Identification does not depend on a title keyword.** Exact identifiers and independently verified supporting evidence establish a Catalog Match. Uncertain listings remain Provisional Candidates until resolved. Every retailer-listed variant remains its own Retailer Listing and result, including apparent duplicates from one Storefront.
5. **Stock stays as fresh as the residential IP safely permits.** Stock Status is exactly `in stock`, `out of stock`, `preorder`, or `unknown`. Results separately disclose the latest status and `Last checked`; stale observations retain their status while monitoring failures appear through Storefront Health. Each active Storefront has fastest-measured-safe Catalog Discovery and Stock Monitoring targets. The full workload must meet those targets or visibly report delays. Different Storefronts may run concurrently within the measured global limit, one job at a time per Storefront, with overlapping work coalesced where possible.
6. **Daily search is local and fast.** The initial page is usable within two seconds over the home network, and search, filter, view, and pagination changes display results within 500 milliseconds under representative full load. One URL-persistent chip input uses match-any semantics across product title, retailer name, and site URL. Flat Offer pagination and Storefront-grouped pagination are both available. Search includes confirmed Offers and Provisional Candidates by default, with Match Status and Stock Status filters and visible provisional evidence.
7. **Purchasing remains manual.** Every result offers a one-click Purchase Handoff to the exact retailer listing. StockHawk never authenticates with retailers, manipulates carts, or performs checkout.
8. **The Health Page makes failures actionable.** It covers every Candidate Site and Storefront; separates health, Catalog Certification, and freshness; shows Connector and URL, last attempt and success, next check and delay, counts and suspicious drops, consecutive failures, latest actionable error, and certification evidence; and provides safe retry, pause/resume, review, and diagnostic-detail controls.
9. **Private Mac mini operation is dependable.** StockHawk runs on the home network through one residential IP, requires one local admin login, and is not directly exposed to the public internet. It starts after reboot, preserves data, and resumes unfinished work without duplicate jobs or manual repair. It creates at least daily database backups, retains the latest seven, and passes a documented restore test before acceptance.
10. **Future alerts have reliable history.** A durable Change Event records every first discovery, disappearance, reappearance, and Stock Status transition, including previous and new values, observation time, and source crawl. V1 stores these events but sends no alert.

Explicit V1 non-goals are automated purchasing or retailer credentials; shipping, pickup, delivery, or purchase-eligibility checks; alert delivery; price history or comparison and extra requests solely for missing prices; proxies, IP rotation, CAPTCHA bypass, or anti-bot evasion; public cloud hosting, multi-user operation, or native mobile apps; private or login-restricted catalogs; brands other than Jellycat; unrelated retailer discovery; and any guarantee about merchandise StockHawk cannot publicly observe.
