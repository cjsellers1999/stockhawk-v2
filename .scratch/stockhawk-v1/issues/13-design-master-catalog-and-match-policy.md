# Design the open-world Master Catalog and Catalog Match policy

Type: grilling
Label: wayfinder:grilling
Status: resolved
Triage: ready-for-human
Blocked by: 03

## Question

Using the accepted V1 completion contract and the primary-source Jellycat research, how should official Master Catalog bootstrap and refresh, exact Product identity, evidence tiers, Catalog Match states, Provisional Candidate resolution, safe alias learning, new and retired Product lifecycle, conflicting evidence, and manual exception review work together? Define exactly when a Retailer Listing becomes a confirmed Offer, what must remain provisional, and which provenance and decision records downstream Connectors and persistence must expose so title keywords and unverified guesses can never poison the Master Catalog.

## Comments

- A Catalog Match may auto-confirm from one verified exact official GTIN/UPC or Jellycat manufacturer code, after proving the retailer field is not an internal SKU. It may also auto-confirm when one retailer's Normalized Title and all known variant facts uniquely match one existing Product, even if that retailer omits the Jellycat brand and manufacturer code. Ambiguity never guesses among exact variants, and affirmative conflicting brand or variant evidence blocks the match.
- Distinct verified official manufacturer codes or GTINs create separate Products by default, even when their names and appearance match. They may share a Product Family; codes become aliases for one Product only when authoritative evidence proves exact equivalence, never from retailer behavior alone.
- The Master Catalog is seeded from official Jellycat surfaces but is not an official-only gate. Products may also be established from independent retailer corroboration or one compatible retailer observation while preserving their lower source authority. Plausible unresolved evidence becomes Provisional, no-signal evidence remains Unclassified, and reliable Jellycat evidence with unresolved variant facts creates a searchable variant-unknown Product.
- V1 has no free-form manual creation of Products or Provisional Candidates. Human input is limited to resolving Retailer Listings StockHawk actually discovered; manual review never invents a retailer Offer, URL, or Stock Status.
- Similar unresolved Provisional Candidates may be placed in one Review Group, but grouping alone never confirms identity; deterministic Normalized Title rules independently handle harmless equivalents such as `Sky Dragon` and `Dragon Sky`. Compatible accepted listings link to one Product while remaining separate Offers, even within one Storefront. A verified official identifier cannot belong to two Products; genuinely different exact variants remain separate.
- A Retailer Listing becomes an Excluded Listing only when retained affirmative evidence shows that it is not Jellycat, such as a conflicting verified brand or an unrelated item. Excluded Listings are hidden from normal potential-Jellycat search but are not deleted. Absence from the Master Catalog is never exclusion evidence, and a material source change automatically reopens the listing for classification.
- Every Product retains a Product Authority explaining why it belongs in the Master Catalog: `official-confirmed` from verified official Jellycat evidence, `retailer-corroborated` from compatible evidence across independent retailers, `retailer-observed` from one retailer's explicit Jellycat surface or unique compatible catalog title, or `review-confirmed` from human resolution of a Retailer Listing StockHawk discovered. Each may serve as a Catalog Match target, but authority never permits an incompatible variant match. Stronger later evidence upgrades the authority while the full prior evidence and decision history remain preserved.
- V1 deliberately favors recall over perfect precision: a small, correctable false-positive margin is preferable to missing Jellycat Products or creating a large review backlog. Compatible normalized titles across two independent retailer organizations are sufficient to establish a `retailer-corroborated` Product without a shared manufacturer identifier. Known size, color, style, or edition differences always create separate Products; title agreement may corroborate identity only when those known variant facts do not conflict.
- A newly discovered Product may also enter the Master Catalog as `retailer-observed` from one Retailer Listing when that Storefront explicitly labels it Jellycat, the listing belongs to a verified Jellycat brand or collection surface, or its Normalized Title is compatible with a known catalog name. It does not require a second retailer or manufacturer identifier. A generic unknown title with no positive Jellycat evidence remains Unclassified rather than entering the Master Catalog or Review Queue.
- Best-effort discovery takes precedence over waiting for exact variant details. When a retailer reliably identifies a Jellycat item but omits the facts needed to choose among known sizes, colors, styles, or editions, StockHawk creates a distinct searchable `variant-unknown` Product and confirms that source listing as its Offer. It never guesses that the listing is one of the known exact variants.
- When later evidence resolves a variant-unknown Offer to one exact Product, StockHawk automatically remaps only that compatible Offer. A variant-unknown Product with no remaining Offers becomes Superseded and disappears from current results, but its source evidence, decisions, mappings, and Change Events remain preserved rather than being deleted.
- The accepted completion contract controls discovery order: an exhaustive bootstrap of accessible official regional catalogs, every exposed exact variant, New/Coming Soon surfaces, and accessible official historical catalogs or PDFs completes before the first large retailer crawl. Afterward, fast official New/Coming Soon observation, periodic complete official scans, retailer Catalog Discovery, and Stock Monitoring run as independent recurring loops; official catalog absence never suppresses retailer discovery.
- Products are never deleted merely because an official source stops showing or retires them. Product Lifecycle is separate from Offer Stock Status and uses `coming-soon`, `current`, `not-currently-observed`, `retired`, or `unknown`; `retired` requires reliable affirmative retirement evidence, while disappearance alone becomes `not-currently-observed`. Retirement never hides a Product or any of its retailer Offers from search.
- Daily search returns actual Retailer Listings, not Master Catalog-only rows. A retired or out-of-stock listing remains searchable normally, but a Product known only to the invisible Master Catalog produces no result until StockHawk discovers at least one real retailer page for it. The retained Product still enables immediate recognition of any future listing.
- Matching uses a deterministic Normalized Title while preserving every retailer's raw title. Harmless differences in capitalization, whitespace, punctuation, quotation marks, common accent drift, equivalent size-unit formatting, and reordered identical name tokens may resolve to the same base name. Normalization never removes or equates conflicting size, color, style, edition, or other meaningful identity facts, so it cannot collapse distinct variants.
- A known catalog name appearing intact inside retailer decoration or translated text may be recognized automatically when known variant facts remain compatible. A fully translated or semantically different title from a reliable Jellycat retailer surface still creates its own searchable `retailer-observed` Product, but AI translation or semantic similarity alone never merges Product identities. Later exact identifiers or corroborating evidence may remap its Offer and Supersede the temporary duplicate.
- Catalog Matches are reversible decisions. Stronger later evidence automatically supersedes a weaker match and remaps only the affected Offer while preserving the former decision and evidence. When equally authoritative evidence conflicts and cannot choose one exact variant, the listing remains searchable under a variant-unknown Product with an Identity Conflict warning; StockHawk never hides it or guesses between conflicting exact identities.
- Manual review is optional cleanup and never blocks Catalog Discovery, Stock Monitoring, classification, or search. Provisional and identity-conflict listings remain searchable while awaiting review. A large or steadily growing Review Queue is treated as an architecture or Connector health failure rather than an expected owner workload; the Health Page must expose its counts, age, inflow, and reasons by Storefront.
- StockHawk intentionally persists every Retailer Listing enumerated during Catalog Discovery, including unrelated and Unclassified Listings. This spends local storage to enable local reclassification, catalog diffing, retained evidence, and fewer residential-IP requests. Unclassified Listings have no current affirmative Jellycat or non-Jellycat evidence: they are not Master Catalog Products, search results, Stock Monitoring targets, or review tasks, but are re-evaluated whenever source content, the Master Catalog, aliases, or classifier rules change.
- The Review Queue is limited to Provisional Candidates with affirmative Jellycat evidence, Identity Conflicts, and suspected duplicate Products that cannot be merged safely by evidence. Excluded Listings, Unclassified Listings, routine retailer-observed Products, and variant-unknown Products without a conflict never create review tasks.
- Review exposes exactly five owner decisions: `link-to-existing-product`, `confirm-discovered-product`, `merge-duplicate-products`, `not-jellycat`, or `decide-later`. Confirmation may use only a Retailer Listing StockHawk discovered; merging changes Product identity while every retailer Offer remains separate. Every action preserves the raw listing, evidence, Stock Observations, Change Events, and superseded decision history.
- Every automated classification, Catalog Match, Product creation, remap, merge, exclusion, and Review Decision emits an immutable Decision Receipt. It records Storefront, source URL and surface, crawl and Connector method, observation time, raw extracted facts and source reference/hash, normalized facts, candidate Products considered, rule or matcher version, chosen authority and reasons, conflicts, actor when human, and links to any previous or superseding decision. Current state is derived from the latest valid decision without erasing history.
- Official regional catalogs contribute a union of source-specific evidence rather than overwriting one another. The same verified Jellycat manufacturer code or GTIN across regions identifies one Product whose regional titles, spellings, measurements, images, aliases, and lifecycle observations are all retained. Different verified official codes remain separate Products even when their names appear identical.
- Any new or changed official Product, alias, or classifier rule first triggers Local Reclassification across all persisted Retailer Listings without network requests. Newly compatible listings are promoted and added to Stock Monitoring, then only affected retailer pages receive targeted evidence or freshness checks. Periodic complete retailer Catalog Discovery remains the independent safety net.
- One retailer's exact Normalized Title plus compatible known variant facts may confirm an Offer against the unique matching Master Catalog Product even when brand and manufacturer code are omitted. A base-name match that cannot distinguish known variants creates a separate searchable variant-unknown Product instead of guessing; an affirmatively conflicting brand prevents either automatic match.
- Each configured official source first uses its normal catalog method and then an approved browser fallback when necessary. The initial bootstrap phase may release retailer discovery only after every official source is accounted for as Certified or explicitly Partial/Blocked with retained evidence. Partial/Blocked sources never contribute false completeness, remain visible in Health, and keep retrying independently while retailer discovery proceeds.
- Each search row uses the Retailer Listing's preserved raw title as its primary title. When the canonical Product name differs, StockHawk shows it secondarily; search indexes raw and Normalized Titles, the canonical name, and trusted aliases.

## Answer

StockHawk V1 uses a recall-first, open-world Master Catalog: missing a real Jellycat is more harmful than admitting a small, correctable false-positive margin. The system therefore promotes strong best-effort evidence quickly, keeps uncertainty visible, and makes every automated or human decision reversible and explainable.

### Discovery and refresh

The initial bootstrap observes every accessible official regional catalog, exposed exact variant, New/Coming Soon surface, and accessible official historical catalog or PDF before the first large retailer crawl. Every configured official source must first be accounted for as Certified or explicitly Partial/Blocked with evidence; normal catalog access precedes the approved browser fallback, and unresolved sources remain visible and retry without freezing retailer discovery.

After bootstrap, fast New/Coming Soon observation, periodic complete official scans, retailer Catalog Discovery, and Stock Monitoring remain independent recurring loops. Official regions contribute a union of evidence: a shared verified Jellycat manufacturer code or GTIN joins regional observations to one Product, while distinct verified official codes remain separate Products. A new Product, alias, or classifier rule first triggers network-free Local Reclassification of all persisted Retailer Listings; only newly relevant pages then receive targeted refreshes, and periodic complete Storefront discovery remains the safety net.

### Stored listing classifications

Catalog Discovery persists every enumerated Retailer Listing so StockHawk can diff catalogs, retain evidence, and reclassify locally instead of spending residential-IP capacity. Each current listing classification is one of:

- **Offer:** the listing has a Catalog Match to a Product, including an explicitly variant-unknown Product when the retailer omits distinguishing facts.
- **Provisional Candidate:** affirmative evidence suggests Jellycat, but there is not enough support for even a best-effort Product decision. It remains searchable with its reason and evidence.
- **Excluded Listing:** affirmative evidence shows another brand or unrelated merchandise. It is hidden but retained and reopens after a material source change.
- **Unclassified Listing:** there is no current affirmative evidence either way. It is stored but is not searchable, monitored for stock, or placed in review; local reclassification revisits it after relevant changes.

Absence from the Master Catalog is never evidence for exclusion.

### Product creation, matching, and identity

Every Product records why it entered the Master Catalog through Product Authority:

- `official-confirmed` from verified official Jellycat evidence;
- `retailer-corroborated` from compatible Normalized Titles and variant facts across two independent retailer organizations;
- `retailer-observed` from one retailer's explicit Jellycat brand/collection surface or a compatible known catalog name; or
- `review-confirmed` from an owner decision on a Retailer Listing StockHawk discovered.

No free-form Product or Offer creation exists. One verified exact official GTIN/UPC or Jellycat manufacturer code may confirm a Catalog Match after its field is proven not to be a retailer-private SKU. A single retailer may also confirm an Offer when its Normalized Title and all known variant facts uniquely match one existing Product, even if the retailer omits both the Jellycat brand and manufacturer code. Two independent retailers with compatible titles may establish a new retailer-corroborated Product without a shared identifier. An affirmative conflicting brand or variant blocks the match.

Different known sizes, colors, styles, editions, or verified official codes are separate Products. They may share a Product Family, but identifier aliases require authoritative proof of exact equivalence. Every retailer-listed entry remains its own Retailer Listing and Offer, including duplicate-looking entries from one Storefront.

When reliable Jellycat evidence omits the facts needed to choose among known variants, StockHawk creates a separate searchable variant-unknown Product instead of guessing or waiting in review. Later exact evidence remaps only the compatible Offer. A variant-unknown Product with no remaining Offers becomes Superseded and leaves current results while all history remains.

### Names and aliases

StockHawk preserves every raw retailer title. Deterministic normalization may reconcile capitalization, spacing, punctuation, quotation marks, common accent drift, equivalent size-unit formatting, reordered identical name tokens, and a known name embedded intact in retailer decoration or translated text. It may never erase or equate conflicting variant facts or other meaningful words. A translation or semantic-AI guess alone cannot merge Product identities; a reliable Jellycat listing with a wholly different or translated title becomes its own best-effort Product until later evidence remaps it.

Search indexes the retailer's raw and Normalized Titles, the Canonical Product Name, and trusted aliases. Each result displays the retailer title first and the canonical Jellycat name secondarily when they differ.

### Correction, lifecycle, and review

Catalog Matches are reversible. Stronger evidence automatically supersedes weaker evidence and remaps only affected Offers. Equally authoritative incompatible evidence keeps the listing searchable under a variant-unknown Product with an Identity Conflict warning rather than guessing. Products are never deleted because an official source removes or retires them. Product Lifecycle is `coming-soon`, `current`, `not-currently-observed`, `retired`, or `unknown`; disappearance alone means not currently observed, and retirement requires affirmative evidence. Real retailer listings remain searchable regardless of Product Lifecycle or Stock Status, while a Master Catalog-only Product creates no search row.

Manual review is optional cleanup and never blocks discovery, monitoring, classification, or search. The Review Queue contains only Provisional Candidates with affirmative Jellycat evidence, Identity Conflicts, and suspected duplicate Products that cannot be merged safely. Its actions are `link-to-existing-product`, `confirm-discovered-product`, `merge-duplicate-products`, `not-jellycat`, and `decide-later`. Merge cannot violate official-identifier or variant identity rules, and it never combines separate Offers. A large or steadily growing queue is a Health failure to diagnose by Storefront, age, inflow, and reason—not an expected owner workload.

### Connector and persistence contract

Every Connector observation must expose enough source-specific data to reproduce classification: Storefront and stable listing identity, URL and discovery surface, parent/variant relationship, raw title and descriptive facts, brand/vendor/category evidence, claimed identifiers with their field meaning, known size/color/style/edition facts, raw availability, observation time, crawl and Connector method/version, and a reference/hash for retained source evidence.

Every classification, Product creation, Catalog Match, remap, merge, exclusion, and Review Decision emits an immutable Decision Receipt containing the raw and normalized evidence, Product candidates considered, matcher/rule version, authority, chosen reasons, conflicts, actor when human, and links to previous or superseding decisions. Persistence derives current state from the latest valid decision while preserving Retailer Listings, Stock Observations, Change Events, mappings, and the complete decision history.
