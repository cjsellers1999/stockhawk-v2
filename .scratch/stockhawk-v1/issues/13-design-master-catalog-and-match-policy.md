# Design the open-world Master Catalog and Catalog Match policy

Type: grilling
Label: wayfinder:grilling
Status: claimed
Triage: ready-for-human
Blocked by: 03

## Question

Using the accepted V1 completion contract and the primary-source Jellycat research, how should official Master Catalog bootstrap and refresh, exact Product identity, evidence tiers, Catalog Match states, Provisional Candidate resolution, safe alias learning, new and retired Product lifecycle, conflicting evidence, and manual exception review work together? Define exactly when a Retailer Listing becomes a confirmed Offer, what must remain provisional, and which provenance and decision records downstream Connectors and persistence must expose so title keywords and unverified guesses can never poison the Master Catalog.

## Comments

- A Catalog Match may auto-confirm from one verified exact official GTIN/UPC or Jellycat manufacturer code, after proving the retailer field is not an internal SKU. Without such an identifier, multiple independent signals must converge on one exact size/color/style/edition. Title, image, URL, or category alone, ambiguity, and conflicting evidence remain Provisional.
- Distinct verified official manufacturer codes or GTINs create separate Products by default, even when their names and appearance match. They may share a Product Family; codes become aliases for one Product only when authoritative evidence proves exact equivalence, never from retailer behavior alone.
- The Master Catalog is seeded from official Jellycat surfaces but is not an official-only gate. A Product may also be established automatically from sufficiently strong evidence that independently corroborates across retailers, while retaining `retailer-corroborated` rather than official source authority. Weak or conflicting retailer-only evidence remains Provisional for later evidence or manual review.
- V1 has no free-form manual creation of Products or Provisional Candidates. Human input is limited to resolving Retailer Listings StockHawk actually discovered; manual review never invents a retailer Offer, URL, or Stock Status.
- Similar Provisional Candidates may be placed in one Review Group, including reordered names such as `Sky Dragon` and `Dragon Sky`, but grouping alone never confirms identity. Compatible accepted listings link to one Product while remaining separate Offers, even within one Storefront. A verified official identifier cannot belong to two Products; genuinely different exact variants remain separate, and insufficient evidence remains unresolved rather than creating a duplicate.
- A Retailer Listing becomes an Excluded Listing only when retained affirmative evidence shows that it is not Jellycat, such as a conflicting verified brand or an unrelated item. Excluded Listings are hidden from normal potential-Jellycat search but are not deleted. Absence from the Master Catalog is never exclusion evidence, and a material source change automatically reopens the listing for classification.
- Every Product retains a Product Authority explaining why it belongs in the Master Catalog: `official-confirmed` from verified official Jellycat evidence, `retailer-corroborated` from strict agreement across independent retailers, or `review-confirmed` from a human resolution of a Retailer Listing StockHawk discovered. Each may serve as a Catalog Match target, but authority never makes a fuzzy title match exact. Stronger later evidence upgrades the authority while the full prior evidence and decision history remain preserved.
- V1 deliberately favors recall over perfect precision: a small, correctable false-positive margin is preferable to missing Jellycat Products or creating a large review backlog. Compatible normalized titles across two independent retailer organizations are sufficient to establish a `retailer-corroborated` Product without a shared manufacturer identifier. Known size, color, style, or edition differences always create separate Products; title agreement may corroborate identity only when those known variant facts do not conflict.
- A newly discovered Product may also enter the Master Catalog as `retailer-observed` from one Retailer Listing when that Storefront explicitly labels it Jellycat or the listing belongs to a verified Jellycat brand or collection surface. It does not require a second retailer or manufacturer identifier. A generic unknown title with no Jellycat evidence remains Provisional rather than entering the Master Catalog automatically.
- Best-effort discovery takes precedence over waiting for exact variant details. When a retailer reliably identifies a Jellycat item but omits the facts needed to choose among known sizes, colors, styles, or editions, StockHawk creates a distinct searchable `variant-unknown` Product and confirms that source listing as its Offer. It never guesses that the listing is one of the known exact variants.
- When later evidence resolves a variant-unknown Offer to one exact Product, StockHawk automatically remaps only that compatible Offer. A variant-unknown Product with no remaining Offers becomes Superseded and disappears from current results, but its source evidence, decisions, mappings, and Change Events remain preserved rather than being deleted.
- The accepted completion contract controls discovery order: an exhaustive bootstrap of accessible official regional catalogs and exact variants completes before the first large retailer crawl. Afterward, official New/Coming Soon observation, periodic complete official scans, retailer Catalog Discovery, and Stock Monitoring run as independent recurring loops; official catalog absence never suppresses retailer discovery.
- Products are never deleted merely because an official source stops showing or retires them. Product Lifecycle is separate from Offer Stock Status and uses `coming-soon`, `current`, `not-currently-observed`, `retired`, or `unknown`; `retired` requires reliable affirmative retirement evidence, while disappearance alone becomes `not-currently-observed`. Retirement never hides a Product or any of its retailer Offers from search.
- Daily search returns actual Retailer Listings, not Master Catalog-only rows. A retired or out-of-stock listing remains searchable normally, but a Product known only to the invisible Master Catalog produces no result until StockHawk discovers at least one real retailer page for it. The retained Product still enables immediate recognition of any future listing.
- Matching uses a deterministic Normalized Title while preserving every retailer's raw title. Harmless differences in capitalization, whitespace, punctuation, quotation marks, common accent drift, equivalent size-unit formatting, and reordered identical name tokens may resolve to the same base name. Normalization never removes or equates conflicting size, color, style, edition, or other meaningful identity facts, so it cannot collapse distinct variants.
- A known catalog name appearing intact inside retailer decoration or translated text may be recognized automatically when known variant facts remain compatible. A fully translated or semantically different title from a reliable Jellycat retailer surface still creates its own searchable `retailer-observed` Product, but AI translation or semantic similarity alone never merges Product identities. Later exact identifiers or corroborating evidence may remap its Offer and Supersede the temporary duplicate.
- Catalog Matches are reversible decisions. Stronger later evidence automatically supersedes a weaker match and remaps only the affected Offer while preserving the former decision and evidence. When equally authoritative evidence conflicts and cannot choose one exact variant, the listing remains searchable under a variant-unknown Product with an Identity Conflict warning; StockHawk never hides it or guesses between conflicting exact identities.
- Manual review is optional cleanup and never blocks Catalog Discovery, Stock Monitoring, classification, or search. Provisional and identity-conflict listings remain searchable while awaiting review. A large or steadily growing Review Queue is treated as an architecture or Connector health failure rather than an expected owner workload; the Health Page must expose its counts, age, inflow, and reasons by Storefront.
- StockHawk intentionally persists every Retailer Listing enumerated during Catalog Discovery, including unrelated and Unclassified Listings. This spends local storage to enable local reclassification, catalog diffing, retained evidence, and fewer residential-IP requests. Unclassified Listings have no current affirmative Jellycat or non-Jellycat evidence: they are not Master Catalog Products, search results, Stock Monitoring targets, or review tasks, but are re-evaluated whenever source content, the Master Catalog, aliases, or classifier rules change.
- The Review Queue is limited to Provisional Candidates with affirmative Jellycat evidence, Identity Conflicts, and suspected duplicate Products that cannot be merged safely by evidence. Excluded Listings, Unclassified Listings, routine retailer-observed Products, and variant-unknown Products without a conflict never create review tasks.
- Review exposes exactly five owner decisions: `link-to-existing-product`, `confirm-discovered-product`, `merge-duplicate-products`, `not-jellycat`, or `decide-later`. Confirmation may use only a Retailer Listing StockHawk discovered; merging changes Product identity while every retailer Offer remains separate. Every action preserves the raw listing, evidence, Stock Observations, Change Events, and superseded decision history.
- Every automated classification, Catalog Match, Product creation, remap, merge, exclusion, and Review Decision emits an immutable Decision Receipt. It records Storefront, source URL and surface, crawl and Connector method, observation time, raw extracted facts and source reference/hash, normalized facts, candidate Products considered, rule or matcher version, chosen authority and reasons, conflicts, actor when human, and links to any previous or superseding decision. Current state is derived from the latest valid decision without erasing history.
