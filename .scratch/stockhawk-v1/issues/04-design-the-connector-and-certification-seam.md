# Design the Connector and Catalog Certification seam

Type: grilling
Label: wayfinder:grilling
Status: resolved
Triage: ready-for-human
Blocked by: 01, 03, 13

## Question

What small Connector interface should hide exhaustive discovery, efficient Stock Monitoring, pagination, normalization, pacing hints, and evidence collection while allowing platform-shared Adapters plus exceptional Storefront overrides? Define the Storefront Integration configuration, Catalog Snapshot result, Catalog Certification evidence, Partial/Blocked behavior, and error contract without leaking platform details to callers.

## Comments

- The design must support genuinely one-off Storefronts whose catalog or stock extraction requires independent code, not merely configuration layered onto a platform-shared implementation.
- StockHawk has one external Connector Interface. A Platform Connector Adapter may serve many compatible Storefronts, while a Bespoke Connector Adapter may contain wholly independent extraction code for one Storefront; both satisfy the same interface and are indistinguishable to the scheduler, persistence, classification, and Health modules. A one-off implementation never creates a one-off external interface.
- The Connector Interface exposes exactly two jobs: Catalog Discovery exhaustively enumerates the public catalog and produces certification evidence, while Stock Monitoring efficiently refreshes known Offers and cannot claim catalog completeness. Either job may preserve stock evidence it naturally encounters. Adapters hide pagination, platform/browser mechanics, and one-off extraction logic; callers never invoke platform-specific methods.
- Both jobs emit bounded observation batches plus an opaque, Adapter-owned Connector Checkpoint so persistence can commit progress and the scheduler can resume after interruption without understanding platform pagination. An Adapter whose source cannot resume safely declares restart-only behavior and replays idempotently from the beginning. Interrupted observations remain stored, but only a successfully completed discovery run may produce a certified Catalog Snapshot.
- Connector Adapters are source translators, not Jellycat classifiers. They emit preserved raw retailer facts plus platform-normalized structure and one of the four Stock Status values while retaining the retailer's original availability evidence. The central catalog matcher alone decides Jellycat identity, Product creation, Catalog Matches, Provisional, Excluded, and Unclassified states, allowing rules to rerun locally without Storefront access.
- The scheduler is the sole owner of outbound pacing across the residential IP. Every Platform and Bespoke Connector Adapter obtains HTTP or browser access through the governed Crawl Request Broker, declares source constraints and server retry hints, and never performs hidden requests, independent sleeps, or bypasses of shared caching and backoff. The scheduler reconciles per-Storefront constraints with the measured global limit.
- Storefront Integration overrides are schema-validated declarative configuration only, such as public roots, locale, supported Adapter options, and initial pacing constraints. They may not contain executable hooks, scripts, or retailer-name branches inside a shared Platform Connector Adapter. Any variation that requires custom behavior belongs in a Bespoke Connector Adapter satisfying the same Connector interface.
- A Storefront Integration pins its audited Adapter and expected public catalog surface. Normal crawling never silently switches Adapters after platform or surface change: the Adapter reports Integration Drift, current Catalog Certification becomes invalid, retained listings and Stock Observations remain available, and Health may suggest a replacement Adapter while repair and re-certification remain explicit.
- Connector Adapters never award their own Catalog Certification or return an unexplained `certified` boolean. A completed discovery submits a versioned Certification Claim containing its catalog routes, expected and observed counts, pagination/end proof, parent and exact-variant closure, snapshot boundary, public-visibility caveats, and missing or conflicting evidence. The central Catalog Certifier applies the common policy to claims from Platform and Bespoke Adapters alike.
- A Partial discovery persists and exposes every valid new observation but never activates a complete replacement Catalog Snapshot or interprets unobserved prior listings as disappeared, removed, or out of stock. The Storefront becomes currently Partial, the previous certified snapshot remains historical evidence rather than current completeness proof, prior listings retain their last-known data, and discovery retries until a new claim earns certification.
- Connector Adapters report expected external and integration problems as typed Connector Failures with stage, code, evidence reference, retained checkpoint, retry safety, and optional server retry time; they do not assign Storefront classifications. Partial means useful observations without completeness proof. Blocked requires a separate Health decision proving a reproducible external barrier after all allowed methods, including approved browser fallback, and repeated ordinary failures alone never promote Partial to Blocked.
- Stock Monitoring accepts known Offer targets while the Adapter chooses efficient bulk, parent-product, or per-listing request shapes. Its terminal result explicitly accounts for every target as observed, confirmed disappeared with evidence, or not observed; not-observed retains the previous Stock Status. Any additional variants encountered become Retailer Listing Observations immediately, but a monitoring job never earns Catalog Certification.
- Catalog Certification requires one consistent Catalog Snapshot boundary. Adapters use a source snapshot/version when available or reconcile counts and repeat the catalog index until stable, retaining start/end times, counts, and content fingerprints. A source whose changing catalog prevents proof of one exhaustive consistent set yields useful Partial observations and a retry, never certification.
- Browser access is an explicit, audited Storefront Integration grant used only when normal public HTTP methods cannot reliably observe the shopper-visible catalog. The grant scopes allowed public routes and the Crawl Request Broker enforces it; Adapters never launch ungranted browser work. V1 does not authorize logins, private catalogs, CAPTCHA interaction or bypass, or anti-bot evasion. A Storefront requiring those methods is retained as Blocked with reproducible evidence; any future authorized-access effort must remain behind the same Connector Interface but is outside this map.
- A versioned Storefront Integration contains only Storefront identity, canonical and approved linked-shop origins, the selected Adapter and configuration version, audited catalog roots, locale/region, schema-validated Adapter options, expected catalog-surface fingerprint, initial pacing constraints, and an optional Browser Access Grant. V1 configurations contain no executable code, credentials, or unrestricted request headers.
- Each Storefront Integration pins a versioned Certification Recipe expressing the method-specific evidence required by its audited Adapter, such as count/cursor closure, product-sitemap plus exact-variant closure, or bespoke route/end proofs. The central Catalog Certifier judges the Adapter's Certification Claim against both common invariants and that recipe; missing required evidence yields Partial, and a recipe change invalidates current certification until fresh Catalog Discovery succeeds.
- Connector Adapters never construct or activate Catalog Snapshots. They stream Retailer Listing Observations and finish with a Certification Claim plus run measurements; the central pipeline persists and classifies observations, evaluates certification, reconciles a consistently bounded set, and only then assembles and activates the Catalog Snapshot.
- Catalog Certification proves exhaustive Storefront enumeration and exact-variant closure, not perfect Jellycat identification. Catalog Snapshot membership records the observed retailer catalog independently from current classification; Local Reclassification may change Unclassified, Provisional, Product, or Offer decisions and their Decision Receipts without recrawling or invalidating Catalog Certification.
- Each committed observation batch is persisted, classified, and made searchable immediately rather than waiting for the full discovery to finish. Results disclose the Storefront's in-progress or Partial coverage state, and valid observations survive later failure. Only a newly certified complete snapshot may reconcile previously known but unseen listings as disappeared.
- Every Retailer Listing Observation carries Storefront-scoped stable source identities for its parent catalog entry and each exact sellable variant. Repeated observations of one identity update that listing idempotently, while distinct source identities remain separate even when titles or details match. When the source exposes no stable identifier, the Adapter derives one from stable source facts and records the derivation rule version as evidence; titles alone never define identity.
- Every Connector run returns Connector Run Metrics from work it already performed, without extra Storefront requests: HTTP/browser operations, bytes, cache/conditional outcomes, active versus scheduler-wait time, latency, retries, rate limits/challenges and retry instructions, parent/variant/target counts, and the access method producing each observation. The scheduler prototype uses these measurements to learn Storefront-specific cost and safe capacity.
- Each Storefront Integration selects one Connector Adapter responsible for both jobs, but that Adapter may internally compose completely independent shared or bespoke discovery, bulk-stock, parent-page, PDP, JSON, or browser mechanisms. Catalog and Stock Monitoring health remain separate: unreliable or unavailable stock evidence yields not-observed targets, preserves prior status and timestamp (or `unknown` if never observed), and surfaces monitoring degradation without guessing availability or automatically invalidating otherwise sound Catalog Certification.
- Both Connector jobs honor scheduler cancellation by safely finishing or abandoning the governed request, flushing the current bounded batch, returning the latest checkpoint and metrics, and cleaning up browser resources. Resume remains idempotent. An unexpected Platform or Bespoke Adapter fault is isolated and reported as a Connector Failure for that Storefront rather than crashing or stalling unrelated jobs.
- Connector Adapters never write directly to StockHawk's database or own evidence-file lifecycle. They emit observations and Source Evidence Artifacts through central persistence interfaces; Decision Receipts reference stable artifact identities and hashes. The persistence frontier may choose storage and retention without changing Platform or Bespoke Adapter behavior.
- The complete external Connector Interface contains only `discoverCatalog(integration, checkpoint, runContext)` and `monitorStock(integration, targets, checkpoint, runContext)`. Both stream bounded observations, checkpoints, and evidence before a typed terminal completion or Connector Failure; discovery completes with a Certification Claim and metrics, monitoring with one outcome per target and metrics. The governed run context exposes only Crawl Request Broker access and cancellation, never persistence, matcher, scheduler, or Health internals.
- V1 loads Platform and Bespoke Connector Adapters from an explicit, type-checked Connector Registry in application code rather than runtime plugins or executable database/file configuration. Storefront Integrations reference a stable Adapter ID and compatible version. A missing Adapter isolates an actionable `adapter-unavailable` Connector Failure to affected Storefronts instead of crashing the application.
- Adapter releases declare whether they affect catalog coverage, variant/source identity, source interpretation, or the Certification Recipe. Internal-only refactors and diagnostics preserve current certification; a coverage-affecting change marks every affected Storefront Integration Needs Recertification and queues fresh discovery. Existing listings remain searchable and the former certificate remains historical evidence, not proof for the changed behavior.
- Every Platform and Bespoke Connector Adapter must pass the same conformance contract before its claims may earn Catalog Certification: stable identities and idempotent replay, batching/checkpoint resume and cancellation, complete monitoring-target accounting, common observations and evidence, typed failures, exclusively governed access, and Certification Claim fixtures matching its recipe. An Adapter under development may emit useful searchable observations, but its Integration remains Partial until conformance and live proof pass.
- A Storefront becomes Dormant only after a certified complete Catalog Snapshot is centrally classified with zero Jellycat Products or Offers. Zero search results, an empty Partial run, or a failed crawl never proves Dormant. Dormant Storefronts remain known and receive infrequent Catalog Discovery; discovering a Jellycat reactivates them and begins normal Stock Monitoring.

## Answer

StockHawk uses one deep Connector module with exactly two external jobs and one common result language. Every normal platform implementation and every genuinely one-off Storefront implementation sits behind this seam, so the scheduler, persistence, matcher, certifier, and Health modules never branch on Shopify, WooCommerce, browser, or retailer-specific details.

### Adapter model

A Storefront Integration selects one registered Connector Adapter responsible for both Catalog Discovery and Stock Monitoring. A Platform Connector Adapter serves multiple compatible Storefronts; a Bespoke Connector Adapter may contain wholly independent code for one exceptional Storefront. Either may internally compose different sitemap, API, parent-page, PDP, JSON, or browser mechanisms for the two jobs. Independent implementation never means an independent external interface.

V1 uses an explicit, type-checked Connector Registry rather than runtime plugins or executable configuration. Storefront Integrations reference stable Adapter IDs and compatible versions. Missing code yields an isolated `adapter-unavailable` Connector Failure for affected Storefronts. Shared and bespoke Adapters must pass the same conformance contract before their claims can certify coverage; an in-development Adapter may still emit searchable Partial observations.

Storefront-specific variation follows a strict ladder: use schema-validated declarative Adapter options first; if custom behavior is required, create a Bespoke Connector Adapter. Shared Adapters contain no retailer-name branches, executable hooks, or scripts from configuration.

### External interface

Conceptually, the complete seam is:

```text
discoverCatalog({ integration, checkpoint, runContext })
  -> observation batches, checkpoints, evidence
  -> Certification Claim + Connector Run Metrics
  -> or Connector Failure

monitorStock({ integration, targets, checkpoint, runContext })
  -> observation batches, checkpoints, evidence
  -> one outcome per target + Connector Run Metrics
  -> or Connector Failure
```

`runContext` exposes only governed Crawl Request Broker access and cancellation. It never exposes persistence, catalog matching, scheduler internals, or Health state. Pagination and source mechanics remain inside the Adapter. Both jobs stream bounded batches and opaque Adapter-owned checkpoints; restart-only sources replay idempotently. Cancellation flushes safe progress, returns the last checkpoint and metrics, and cleans up browser resources. An Adapter fault is isolated to its Storefront.

Adapters never write StockHawk tables or manage evidence files. They emit Retailer Listing Observations and Source Evidence Artifacts to central persistence. Each observation includes stable Storefront-scoped parent and exact-variant identities, preserved source facts, platform-normalized structure, the normalized four-state Stock Status plus original availability evidence, origin and time, and identity-rule version when an ID must be derived. Titles never define source identity, and distinct retailer entries remain distinct.

Connector Adapters translate sources; they do not decide Jellycat identity, Product creation, Catalog Matches, or Provisional, Excluded, and Unclassified states. The central matcher owns those decisions and may rerun them locally without Storefront requests.

### Storefront Integration and access

A Storefront Integration is versioned declarative data containing Storefront identity, canonical and approved linked-shop origins, Adapter ID/configuration version, audited catalog roots, locale/region, validated Adapter options, expected catalog-surface fingerprint, Certification Recipe, initial pacing constraints, and an optional Browser Access Grant. V1 stores no Connector code, credentials, or unrestricted headers in it.

Every HTTP and browser action passes through the Crawl Request Broker. Adapters supply source constraints and server retry hints but never send hidden requests, sleep independently, or bypass shared caching, backoff, and global/per-Storefront pacing. Browser use is opt-in per audited Storefront, scoped to approved public routes, and allowed only when normal public HTTP methods are insufficient. V1 does not authorize credentials, private catalogs, CAPTCHA interaction or bypass, or anti-bot evasion; a Storefront requiring them is retained as Blocked with evidence. A later authorized-access effort can reuse the same Connector interface without changing catalog callers.

The Integration pins its audited Adapter and expected surface. Platform or catalog-surface change produces Integration Drift, invalidates current certification, retains all catalog and stock history, and may suggest—but never silently applies—a replacement Adapter. Coverage-affecting Adapter, identity, interpretation, configuration, or Certification Recipe changes similarly mark affected Integrations Needs Recertification; internal-only refactors do not.

### Discovery and certification

Catalog Discovery enumerates the entire observable public retailer catalog and every exact sellable variant, not merely Jellycat search or brand results. Each committed batch becomes immediately persistable, classifiable, and searchable, so an in-stock product found early does not wait for a long crawl. The Adapter finishes by submitting a versioned Certification Claim rather than awarding itself certification.

The Storefront Integration's versioned Certification Recipe states method-specific proof—such as API total/cursor closure, product-sitemap and exact-variant closure, or bespoke route/end conditions. The central Catalog Certifier applies both common invariants and that recipe to the claim. Required evidence includes enumerated routes, expected and observed counts, pagination/end proof, parent and variant closure, a consistent snapshot boundary, source fingerprints, public-visibility caveats, gaps, and conflicts.

Adapters use a source snapshot/version when available or repeat and reconcile the catalog index until stable. The central pipeline persists and classifies all batches, evaluates the claim, and only then assembles and activates a complete Catalog Snapshot. Certification proves exhaustive retailer enumeration, not perfect Jellycat classification; Local Reclassification can change Product and Offer decisions without altering snapshot membership or spending network requests.

A Partial run preserves and exposes every valid observation but does not activate a complete replacement snapshot or interpret unseen prior listings as removed or out of stock. Current coverage becomes Partial, the previous certified snapshot remains historical evidence, and retries continue. Only a new certified snapshot may perform catalog-wide disappearance reconciliation. A Storefront becomes Dormant only when a certified complete snapshot is classified with zero Jellycat Products or Offers; empty searches and incomplete runs cannot prove Dormant.

### Stock, failures, and measurement

Stock Monitoring receives known Offer targets while the Adapter selects the cheapest source request shape. It must account for every target as observed, confirmed disappeared with evidence, or not observed. Failed, omitted, or intrinsically unobservable stock evidence retains the prior status and timestamp, or remains `unknown` when never observed; StockHawk never guesses. Additional variants encountered are emitted immediately, but monitoring never certifies catalog coverage. Discovery and monitoring health remain independent, so degraded stock access does not automatically erase sound catalog proof.

Expected source and configuration problems return typed Connector Failures with stable code, stage, evidence reference, retained checkpoint, retry safety, and optional server retry time. Adapters never classify a Storefront Partial or Blocked. Partial requires useful observations without sufficient completeness proof; Blocked is a separate Health decision requiring a reproducible external barrier after all V1-allowed methods. Repeated ordinary failures alone never promote Partial to Blocked.

Every run emits Connector Run Metrics without extra Storefront requests: HTTP/browser operations, bytes, cache and conditional outcomes, active and scheduler-wait time, latency, retries, rate limits/challenges and retry instructions, item/variant/target counts, and access method. These measurements are the input to the residential-IP scheduler prototype, allowing it to learn safe cost and capacity rather than guess.
