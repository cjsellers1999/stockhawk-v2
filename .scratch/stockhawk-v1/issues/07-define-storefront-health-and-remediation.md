# Define Storefront Health and remediation

Type: grilling
Label: wayfinder:grilling
Status: claimed
Triage: ready-for-human
Blocked by: 04, 05

## Question

Which independent health, coverage, and freshness states must StockHawk maintain so that working requests, Catalog Certification, stale observations, broken Connectors, blocked access, Dormant Storefronts, Dead Storefronts, and Non-Store Candidates cannot be confused? Define transition rules, failure thresholds, diagnostics, recovery behavior, stale-data treatment, and the information/actions the Health Page must expose for implementation fixes.

## Comments

- **Decision — goal and cadence remain independent:** Stock Freshness Goal is the desired maximum age of trustworthy Current Stock State, while Expected Check Cadence is the scheduler's measured safe expectation for that Storefront and access method. A goal miss degrades freshness visibly but never authorizes unsafe traffic, overrides Retry-After/backoff, or lets the system redefine “fresh” by silently weakening the goal. Access may remain healthy while freshness is degraded because the safe expected cadence cannot meet the goal.
- **Decision — overdue work coalesces:** A Storefront has at most one pending Stock Monitoring job, and newly due Offer targets merge into it. Missing several freshness intervals increases the job's lateness and priority but never creates one queued copy per missed interval. Safe Storefront and residential-IP pacing still wins; a successful observation resets that Offer's age and calculates its next due time from the new observation.
- **Owner target and scale:** V1's desired Stock Freshness Goal is 15 minutes. Treat this as a visible objective rather than a guarantee that overrides safe access. Design and later verification must support at least 100,000 independently monitored Offers—not merely 100,000 canonical Products—because the same Product at different Storefronts has separate Stock Status, evidence, and freshness.
- **Decision — prioritize restock detection under constrained capacity:** The 15-minute objective is specifically the Restock Detection Goal for Offers last observed `out of stock`. Offers last observed `in stock` use a separate slower-but-bounded In-Stock Verification Goal because the owner accepts some stale positive rows in exchange for reducing the chance of missing short restocks. This priority changes request selection only when source work is separable and capacity is constrained: any bulk, parent, or Storefront response that can refresh other due Offers at no additional source cost updates them all. In-stock Offers are never abandoned, and every result exposes its observation age.
- **Decision — in-stock verification target:** The In-Stock Verification Goal is 60 minutes. It is a maximum desired observation age, not a minimum wait: spare safe capacity and shared/bulk responses may refresh an in-stock Offer sooner, while misses become visible freshness degradation rather than unsafe traffic.
- **Decision — preorder verification target:** Offers last observed `preorder` use the same 60-minute verification goal as `in stock`. Preorder volume and transition behavior are measured so a later evidence-based policy may tighten the goal without changing the status model; absent or rare preorders consume no special reserved capacity.
- **Decision — unknown recovery:** A newly discovered Offer whose Current Stock State is `unknown` receives a 15-minute Unknown Recovery Goal while its audited source is expected to expose stock. Failed checks never reset a previously trustworthy Current Stock State to `unknown`; they preserve its value and timestamp. Repeated evidence that stock is intrinsically unobservable ends accelerated retries, retains `unknown`, and creates visible Stock Monitoring degradation pending Integration repair or scheduled re-audit.
- **UI requirement — investigate unknowns:** Normal Offer search retains its `unknown` Stock Status filter. The Health Page aggregates unknown target count and share by Storefront and stable reason, including age, last attempt, expected cadence, next retry, access method, and diagnostic evidence. Each aggregate links to the exact affected Offer rows and their retailer pages so the owner can investigate without treating an unknown status as a guessed stock answer.
- **Decision — unknowns degrade monitoring, not access:** An unknown Offer immediately remains visible at the Offer level and contributes to Stock Monitoring Health coverage, but it does not by itself make Storefront Health unhealthy. The Health Page reports conclusive targets over eligible targets, unknown count/share, age, and reasons independently from access and Catalog Certification; material or persistent unknowns degrade the monitoring dimension while successful Storefront access remains accurately healthy.
- **Decision — independent truth with derived attention:** Health storage and transitions keep Candidate Site resolution, Storefront disposition, Storefront access, Catalog coverage/certification, Stock Monitoring Health, and Offer freshness independent. The Health Page may derive one Attention Severity for sorting and filtering, but it never replaces those states or acts as transition evidence. A row always exposes the contributing dimensions and reasons.
- **Domain correction — lifecycle is not access:** A Non-Store Candidate remains a Candidate Site resolution and never becomes a Storefront. Dormant describes a live Storefront with a certified zero-Jellycat catalog, while Blocked describes a live Storefront with a proven allowed-access barrier; neither is interchangeable with Dead. The Health model must allow these facts to coexist rather than force them into one mutually exclusive `live/dormant/dead/blocked` field.
- **Decision — tolerate isolated failures, surface structural failures:** One ordinary transient request or run failure is retained with its diagnostics and retry plan but does not immediately degrade access while recent success remains trustworthy. Repeated failures or excessive time without success may degrade the affected job dimension. Deterministic structural failures such as an unavailable Adapter, invalid Integration configuration, or Integration Drift degrade the affected Integration immediately because retrying unchanged work cannot repair them.
- **Decision — throttling is capacity evidence, not health failure:** A normal rate limit, `Retry-After`, or equivalent throttle never increments the ordinary consecutive-failure threshold or degrades Storefront access by itself. It updates Storefront pacing, backoff, Expected Check Cadence, and capacity diagnostics. If safe throttling prevents a Stock Freshness Goal from being met, the freshness dimension exposes that shortfall; only a prolonged absence of any successful allowed access or separate proof of a reproducible barrier can change access health.
- **Decision — access degradation combines count and time:** Ordinary access degradation is never triggered by a raw failure count alone. It combines consecutive scheduled non-throttle failures, elapsed time since the last representative success, the affected job's Expected Check Cadence and freshness/coverage obligations, and typed failure class. This prevents rapid retries during one incident from manufacturing severity while ensuring a long success gap cannot remain hidden because backoff produced few attempts.
- **Decision — four access states per job dimension:** Catalog Discovery access and Stock Monitoring access independently use `unassessed`, `healthy`, `degraded`, or `blocked`. `Unassessed` has no representative current evidence; `healthy` has allowed access behaving as expected; `degraded` is unreliable, incomplete, or structurally broken without proof of an external barrier; and `blocked` requires a reproducible external barrier after all V1-allowed methods. Dead/Dormant disposition, Partial/Certified coverage, and recovering presentation remain separate facts.
- **Decision — ordinary degradation threshold:** Except for deterministic structural failures that degrade immediately, `healthy` access becomes `degraded` after three consecutive scheduled non-throttle jobs fail without representative success and those failures span at least two Expected Check Cadences. Internal request retries belong to their one scheduled job, rate limits remain excluded, and a homepage or unrelated endpoint cannot reset the count; success must exercise the affected catalog or stock route. Freshness may degrade independently before this access threshold is crossed.
- **Decision — evidence-matched access recovery:** For Stock Monitoring access, one representative success displays `recovering` and a second consecutive scheduled success returns canonical access health to `healthy`; an intervening ordinary failure resets recovery progress. Catalog Discovery access returns healthy after one complete representative discovery exercises all required routes, while Catalog Certification remains independently judged. Structural degradation additionally requires a repaired Storefront Integration version, so unchanged broken configuration cannot recover through retries alone. `Recovering` is presentation of transition progress, not a fifth canonical state.
- **Decision — strict automatic Blocked classification:** Health may assign `blocked` without owner approval only when separated attempts reproduce the same specific external barrier after required backoff, every V1-authorized method in the Storefront Integration—including an applicable Browser Access Grant—has been exercised, and retained evidence identifies the barrier. Rate limits, timeouts, ordinary failures, missing or broken Adapter code, invalid configuration, and Integration Drift are insufficient. The Storefront and listings remain retained, and Health exposes the evidence, retailer link, diagnostics, and safe retry/remediation controls.
- **Decision — Blocked recheck and recovery:** A Blocked access dimension receives the least-cost representative recheck after 6 hours, then 24 hours, 72 hours, and weekly while the barrier persists; a later server instruction always wins. `Retry now` queues the earliest broker-safe attempt rather than bypassing backoff. Successful access enters the accepted recovery path, while Catalog Certification remains unavailable until fresh complete discovery separately earns it.
- **Decision — strict Dead Storefront boundary:** Connector failures, throttles, CAPTCHA, maintenance, TLS problems, and ordinary downtime never establish death. A Storefront becomes Dead only from affirmative permanent-closure evidence or three fresh Storefront Audits spanning at least 30 days that reproduce terminal disappearance such as DNS nonexistence and find no redirect, retailer-controlled replacement shop, or merely temporary condition. Until then access remains degraded or Blocked. A relocated commerce destination produces Integration repair/migration rather than death.
- **Decision — Dead re-audit and revival:** Dead Storefronts leave normal Catalog Discovery and Stock Monitoring but receive a least-cost monthly Storefront re-audit and expose `Re-audit now`. A returned response, redirect, or retailer-controlled commerce destination creates a visible revival/repair task; StockHawk performs a fresh Storefront Audit, publishes a current Integration, and runs new Catalog Discovery rather than silently restoring the old Adapter or certification.
- **Decision — Non-Store Candidates are manual-recheck-only:** A freshly audited Candidate Site that neither exposes a retail catalog nor resolves to a retailer-controlled Storefront leaves the active Seed List and receives no recurring automatic crawl or re-audit. Its Seed Site Records, resolution, and evidence remain preserved under resolved Candidate outcomes rather than unhealthy Storefronts, and an explicit owner `Re-audit` action can reopen it if circumstances change.
- **Decision — Dormant change detection and safety net:** A Dormant Storefront with a trustworthy low-cost catalog-change signal checks that signal daily and queues immediate complete Catalog Discovery when it changes. Every Dormant Storefront also receives complete discovery at least weekly regardless of the signal; one without a trustworthy cheap signal uses that weekly discovery alone. Discovery of any Jellycat Offer immediately ends Dormant disposition and starts normal Stock Monitoring.
- **Decision — active catalog discovery target:** An active Jellycat Storefront checks a trustworthy low-cost catalog-change signal hourly and queues immediate complete Catalog Discovery when it changes. Every active Storefront also has a 24-hour complete-discovery goal regardless of that signal; one without a trustworthy cheap signal runs complete discovery directly. New observations become searchable immediately as Partial progress, while safe source/IP pacing still wins and missed goals become visible catalog-freshness degradation.
- **Decision — Partial limits completeness confidence only:** A Partial Storefront does not hide, quarantine, or reduce the usability of listings already found. Those listings remain in normal search with their independently determined Stock Status and retailer purchase link. Partial warns that additional listings may still be undiscovered; an incomplete run never treats absent prior listings as removed or out of stock.
- **Decision — certification history and freshness are separate:** A past Catalog Certification and its evidence remain immutable and visible when they age; missing the applicable discovery goal degrades Catalog Freshness instead of deleting that proof. A newer incomplete discovery makes current coverage Partial while retaining the prior certification as history, and a coverage-affecting Integration or recipe change separately enters Needs Recertification. None of these states hides known listings.
- **Decision — Listing Presence is separate from stock:** A Retailer Listing has an `active` or `inactive` presence lifecycle independent of `in stock`, `out of stock`, `preorder`, or `unknown`. One missing crawl only records suspected disappearance. Absence from two complete certified Catalog Snapshots plus direct listing-page disappearance evidence establishes inactive; positive evidence immediately reactivates it. Inactive listings are never deleted and remain historically searchable, but do not appear in current in-stock purchasing results. A Partial run cannot change presence or infer stock from absence.
- **Decision — inactive Offers leave frequent monitoring:** An inactive Offer does not consume 15- or 60-minute Stock Monitoring capacity. Normal Storefront catalog-change checks and complete Catalog Discovery remain responsible for detecting its return; any positive observation immediately reactivates the listing and resumes the appropriate Stock Monitoring goal. Historical data and search access remain retained.
- **Decision — stale stock remains usable and explicit:** A stale Current Stock State remains under its last trustworthy Stock Status filter rather than being hidden or reset to `unknown`. Every result exposes `Last checked` age and a stale treatment when its applicable freshness goal is missed; fresh matches rank before stale matches by default. Failed checks remain separate diagnostic facts, and manual Purchase Handoff remains available.
- **UI requirement — Storefront overview:** The Health Page shows one row per Storefront, ordered by derived Attention Severity so actionable problems appear first without hiding healthy stores. Every row displays its independent access, catalog coverage/freshness, Stock Monitoring, disposition, affected counts, and next retry or action. Filters include All, Needs Attention, Healthy, Dormant, and Dead; row drill-down exposes evidence, affected Offers, attempts, timing, retailer links, and safe remediation actions.
- **Decision — attention follows buying impact:** Derived Attention Severity prioritizes current purchasing impact and repair leverage rather than alphabetical or fixed state ordering. Problems affecting many active Offers, especially overdue restock-detection targets, rank above routine Dormant or Dead maintenance; deterministic structural failures rank highly when one repair can restore broad coverage. Lower-ranked Storefronts remain visible and canonical states remain unchanged.
- **Decision — health is evidence-driven, not owner-painted:** The owner cannot directly mark canonical access, coverage, monitoring, or freshness healthy. Health Page controls instead queue broker-safe Stock Monitoring, complete Catalog Discovery, or Storefront Audit work and expose source evidence and affected Offers; successful representative evidence drives the accepted transition automatically. Manual controls never bypass backoff or substitute an assertion for verification.
- **V1 scope — no manual pause:** The owner does not need a Storefront, Catalog Discovery, or Stock Monitoring Pause/Resume feature. The Crawl Request Broker's automatic pacing and backoff, plus normal Storefront lifecycle scheduling, remain the traffic controls; the Health Page does not add a manual pause state or queue.
- **Decision — automatic recovery versus Repair Required:** Transient failures, timeouts, throttles, and temporary source problems recover through automatic broker-safe pacing, backoff, and retries without owner intervention. A deterministic structural failure that unchanged work cannot repair stops repeated execution of the affected job and exposes derived `Repair Required` diagnostics and next steps. Publishing a repaired Integration queues representative verification; successful evidence, not the repair assertion, changes canonical health.
- **Decision — local downtime is a Collection Gap:** Time when StockHawk or its Mac Mini is offline never counts as Storefront or Connector failure and cannot degrade Storefront access health. Catalog and stock ages continue honestly, the Health Page exposes the system-level Collection Gap, and missed work coalesces under the accepted scheduler rule. Restart catch-up prioritizes overdue restock detection while the Crawl Request Broker preserves safe source pacing.
- **UI requirement — system banner and throughput:** Above Storefront rows, the Health Page shows collector heartbeat, overdue Offer count, oldest overdue restock target, and whether residential-IP capacity is constrained. It also shows rolling one-minute Collection Throughput separately as actual source requests per second, conclusive Offer refreshes per second, and conclusive restock-target refreshes per second. A bulk response may increment many Offer counters from one request; request rate is IP pressure while Offer rates are useful work.
- **UI requirement — backlog trend and honest catch-up:** The system banner reports whether the overdue backlog is growing or shrinking and its recent net rate. It estimates catch-up time only when current safe throughput and incoming work make an estimate credible; unstable or capacity-deficient conditions instead say that the applicable freshness goal is currently unreachable. Queue count alone is never presented as progress.
- **Decision — monitoring coverage is an exact score:** Stock Monitoring Health reports eligible active Offers with a trustworthy conclusive Current Stock State over all eligible active Offers, plus the exact linked unknown remainder. It uses no arbitrary binary cutoff; gaps influence Attention Severity according to count, share, age, and shared cause. Freshness Compliance separately reports how many conclusive answers meet their applicable freshness goal, so understandable-but-stale data cannot be confused with recent attempts that remain unknown.
- **UI requirement — freshness is split by operational goal:** Health never presents one blended freshness percentage that lets numerous lower-priority checks conceal overdue restock detection. It separately reports Offers last observed out of stock that meet the 15-minute Restock Detection Goal, in-stock Offers that meet the 60-minute In-Stock Verification Goal, preorder Offers under their accepted 60-minute goal, and unknown targets under Unknown Recovery. This indicates whether StockHawk can detect a newly returned item promptly, not merely whether it is busy.
- **UI requirement — Search Health Warning:** Material collection degradation that can delay current purchasing results produces a concise warning above normal Offer search, naming the affected goal, overdue scale, and oldest age with a link to Health details. Search results remain available under their preserved statuses and ages. When operation is normal, no large banner consumes the daily search interface.
- **UI requirement — distinguish automatic from owner remediation:** Needs Attention includes both observable problems StockHawk is already handling and structural problems needing implementation work. Retryable cases show derived `Auto-Recovering` with stable reason and next safe attempt; deterministic structural cases show `Repair Required` with evidence and repair guidance. Neither presentation becomes a canonical access state, and only Repair Required implies owner action.
- **Decision — remediation controls are idempotent:** Repeated `Retry`, `Run discovery`, or `Re-audit` actions never create duplicate work or bypass pacing. They merge targets and intent into the existing Storefront job and display its current state and earliest broker-safe run time. This applies across repeated clicks, page refreshes, and application restarts.
- **UI requirement — correlated Health timeline:** Each Storefront drill-down presents a chronological timeline of representative success, failure, throttle, backoff, health transition, Integration change, and remediation evidence rather than only its latest label. Verbose diagnostics follow the accepted rolling 30-day retention window; compact reasoned transitions and causal envelopes remain permanent under the persistence policy.
- **Decision — derived Healthy view is strict but non-authoritative:** An active Storefront appears in Healthy only when catalog and stock access are healthy, catalog coverage is currently certified and fresh, Stock Monitoring coverage is complete for eligible active Offers, and all applicable freshness obligations are met. The view is derived from those facts rather than stored as one health state. Optional presentation data, especially a missing product image, never affects health, certification, presence, classification, or stock.
