StockHawk table map

Thirteen tables and one view, grouped by job. Read “belongs to” as “contains a foreign key pointing at.”

Identity: stable nouns

storefront

One retailer website, such as a particular shop origin.

Parent of many retailer_listing rows.

product

One canonical StockHawk product and variant.

Connected to retailer pages through catalog_match.

retailer_listing

One stable product-page identity on one storefront.

Belongs to storefront; owns listing and stock timelines.

Intake and provenance

observation_batch

One idempotent import command/run.

Parent of listing observations, stock observations, and change events.

source_evidence_artifact

A hashed source URL captured at a known time.

Proves listing observations, stock observations, and catalog matches.

Historical facts and decisions

retailer_listing_observation

A time-stamped snapshot of title, purchase URL, image, and page observation.

Belongs to one batch, evidence artifact, and retailer listing.

stock_observation

A time-stamped stock fact: in stock, out of stock, preorder, or unknown.

Belongs to one batch, evidence artifact, and retailer listing.

catalog_match

An evidence-backed decision that a retailer listing represents a canonical product.

Joins retailer_listing to product; only one active match per listing.

change_event

An ordered business event: discovered, reappeared, or stock changed.

Cites its product, listing, batch, and causal observation rows.

Current-state pointers

current_listing_state

One row per listing pointing to the listing observation that currently wins.

Primary key is also a foreign key to retailer_listing.

current_stock_state

One row per listing pointing to the stock observation that currently wins.

Copies the winning observation facts so a composite foreign key proves they agree.

Read model

search_document_source (view, not a table)

Joins current listing state, current stock, active product match, storefront, and product.

A saved SELECT: it stores the recipe, not independent rows.

search_document

One denormalized Offer row per retailer listing for simple, fast search.

Rebuilt from the source view; references product, storefront, and listing.

Operations

service_heartbeat

The latest observed heartbeat time for each backend service name.

Independent of the catalog graph.

Cardinality cheat sheet

storefront          1 ──── * retailer_listing
retailer_listing    1 ──── * retailer_listing_observation
retailer_listing    1 ──── * stock_observation
retailer_listing    1 ──── * catalog_match history
retailer_listing    1 ──── 0..1 active catalog_match
retailer_listing    1 ──── 0..1 current_listing_state
retailer_listing    1 ──── 0..1 current_stock_state
retailer_listing    1 ──── 0..1 search_document
product             1 ──── * catalog_match
observation_batch   1 ──── * observations and change_event
source_evidence     1 ──── * observations and catalog_match

When designing your next table

  1. Say its role in one sentence.
  2. Name the stable owner it belongs to.
  3. Decide whether rows are history or mutable current state.
  4. Add a foreign key for every claimed relationship.
  5. Add uniqueness for every “only one” sentence.
  6. Add indexes for real lookup paths, not decoration.
